# Pakistan Tourism & Heritage Reference

## Source

- Pakistan Tourism Development Corporation (PTDC), Government of Pakistan
- PTDC / National Tourism official website: `https://tourism.gov.pk`
- Provincial tourism bodies (e.g., Tourism Development Corporation of Punjab) operate separately and may have their own destination listings and services distinct from PTDC's federal promotion.
- For formally UNESCO-inscribed sites specifically, see the separate `pakistan-unesco-heritage-sites` skill and its authoritative UNESCO source.

## Major Tourism Regions

- Gilgit-Baltistan: home to several of the world's "eight-thousander" peaks and more than fifty peaks above 7,000m; major hub for mountaineering, trekking, and high-altitude adventure tourism.
- Khyber Pakhtunkhwa's northern valleys: Swat, Chitral, and the Kalash valleys, known for scenic and cultural tourism.
- Azad Jammu & Kashmir: mountain and lake tourism.
- Punjab: historic cities (Lahore and others) with Mughal-era architecture, forts, and shrines.
- Sindh and Balochistan: coastal tourism, archaeological sites, and desert landscapes.

## Activity Types

- Adventure/nature tourism: trekking, mountaineering, white-water rafting, jeep safaris, wildlife/national-park visits.
- Cultural/historic tourism: forts, shrines, mosques, colonial and Mughal-era monuments.
- Urban tourism: major cities noted for their cultural and historic character.

## Extraction Notes

- Do not assume a well-known tourist site has UNESCO World Heritage status; confirm against `pakistan-unesco-heritage-sites` if that specific claim matters.
- Treat destination logistics (fees, access, seasonal conditions) as time-sensitive; direct users to current provincial tourism department sources for up-to-date practical details.
- Distinguish PTDC's federal-level role from provincial tourism development bodies' own destination promotion and services.
