# University Admissions Reference

## Source

- Individual university admission portals (e.g., `admissions.pu.edu.pk`, `admissions.uet.edu.pk`, `numl.edu.pk/admissions`, etc.)
- Provincial admission authorities (e.g., Punjab's centralized engineering/medical admission portal, Sindh's centralized MBBS/BDS admission)
- HEC's entry test policy page (`hec.gov.pk`) — lists mandatory tests (NET, MDCAT, ECAT, HAT-HEC)
- Private university admission pages (LUMS, NUST, FAST, IoBM, etc.)

## Entry Test Definitions

| Test | Full Name | Used For | Administered By |
|------|-----------|----------|----------------|
| MDCAT | Medical and Dental College Admission Test | MBBS, BDS admission | PMDC / provincial health departments |
| NMDCAT | National MDCAT | Federal/provincial medical colleges | PMC (Pakistan Medical Commission) |
| ECAT | Engineering College Admission Test | Engineering programs (Punjab) | UET Lahore / Punjab |
| NET | NUST Entry Test | NUST undergraduate programs | NUST |
| HAT-HEC | HEC Admission Test | Various universities (HEC-affiliated) | HEC / NTS |
| LAT | Law Admission Test | LLB programs | HEC |

## Admission Categories

- Open Merit — general competition, no quota
- Provincial/Regional Quota — reserved for students domiciled in a specific province/region
- Federal Quota — reserved for federal territory domiciles
- Overseas/Foreign Quota — for overseas Pakistanis or foreign nationals
- Disabled Quota — reserved for students with disabilities
- Sports Quota — reserved for sports achievers
- Minority Quota — reserved for religious minorities
- Self-Finance/Self-Support — higher fee, often lower merit threshold

## Extraction Notes

- Closing merit is the aggregate score (typically a percentage) of the last student admitted to a program in a given category.
- Minimum eligibility is the threshold to apply — usually lower than the closing merit.
- Merit is typically calculated from: Matric/O-Level (10-15%), Intermediate/A-Level (30-40%), Entry Test (45-55%) — exact weightages vary by university and program.
- Medical college merit weightage is regulated by PMC; engineering merit weightage varies by province/university.
- Private universities may use their own criteria — always check the specific university's admission policy.
- Admission cycles in Pakistan typically run: Fall (September–October intake) and Spring (February–March intake) — closing merits differ between cycles.
