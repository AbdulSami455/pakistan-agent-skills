# NHMP Road Safety

## Overview

Use this skill for Pakistan's National Highways & Motorway Police (NHMP), the specialized force responsible for traffic enforcement and safety on Pakistan's motorway and national highway network. This is a jurisdiction/process reference skill for road-safety questions — not a live traffic-condition tracker and not the National Highway Authority (which builds/maintains road infrastructure rather than policing it).

## Coverage

- Jurisdiction: NHMP was formed in 1997 to police Pakistan's newly built motorway network (starting with the M-2), and was expanded in 2001 to also patrol national highways (starting with the N-5), giving it the combined "National Highways & Motorway Police" name and mandate.
- Enforcement functions: speed-limit enforcement (including speed cameras), general traffic-law compliance, accident response and recovery on the motorway/highway network, and security patrolling.
- Public safety services: a 24-hour nationwide helpline (commonly cited as 130, accessible from mobile and landline), and an FM radio service (FM-95) broadcasting road-safety information and traffic advisories to motorists.
- Distinction from city traffic police: NHMP's jurisdiction is specifically the motorway and national highway network; provincial/city traffic police (e.g., Islamabad Traffic Police, and other city forces) separately handle urban road traffic within city limits — the two are not the same force.
- Distinction from National Highway Authority (NHA): NHA is the body responsible for planning, building, and maintaining the physical national highway/motorway infrastructure; NHMP is the police force enforcing traffic law on that infrastructure — a construction/maintenance body versus a law-enforcement body.

## Use This Skill For

- explaining NHMP's general jurisdiction (motorways and national highways specifically, not city roads)
- describing NHMP's enforcement functions (speed enforcement, accident response) and public safety services (helpline, FM radio advisories)
- clarifying the difference between NHMP (traffic policing) and NHA (infrastructure building/maintenance)
- clarifying the difference between NHMP (motorway/highway jurisdiction) and city/provincial traffic police (urban jurisdiction)
- providing general road-safety context for someone planning motorway travel in Pakistan

## When Not to Use This Skill

- For road/highway infrastructure statistics (network length, project status) — that would fall under NHA or general infrastructure sources, not this policing-focused skill.
- For city/urban traffic rules or violations — those fall under the relevant provincial/city traffic police, a separate jurisdiction from NHMP.
- For live/current road-condition or traffic-incident status — direct to NHMP's official current channels rather than asserting real-time conditions from this structural reference.

## Routing Rules

- Use this skill for NHMP's specific jurisdiction, enforcement role, and public safety services on motorways/national highways.
- If the question is about urban/city traffic rules, clarify that NHMP's jurisdiction does not extend there and that a separate city/provincial traffic police force applies.
- If the question is about infrastructure (road-building, network expansion) rather than traffic policing, clarify that this is NHA's domain, not NHMP's.

## Extraction Workflow

1. Identify whether the question concerns motorway/national-highway travel (NHMP jurisdiction) or urban/city travel (separate traffic police jurisdiction).
2. If asking about a specific enforcement mechanism (e.g., speed cameras), describe it as part of NHMP's general enforcement toolkit rather than asserting specific current camera locations/limits without flagging the need for current verification.
3. For safety-service questions (helpline, FM radio), describe the general service rather than treating a specific frequency/number as unchangeable without noting it should be confirmed against NHMP's current official information.
4. If the question conflates NHMP with NHA, clarify the policing-versus-infrastructure distinction before answering.

## Technical Rules

- NHMP's jurisdiction is specifically the motorway and national highway network, not city/urban roads — do not describe it as a general national traffic-police force covering all roads.
- Keep NHMP (traffic law enforcement) distinct from NHA (infrastructure planning/construction/maintenance); they are separate bodies with different mandates despite the overlapping "National Highway" naming.
- Treat specific operational details (helpline number, FM frequency, speed limits) as needing confirmation against NHMP's current official information rather than presented as permanently fixed.

## Validation Checklist

- Confirm whether the question concerns motorway/highway (NHMP) or urban/city (separate traffic police) jurisdiction.
- Verify NHMP is not being conflated with NHA's infrastructure-building role.
- Flag specific operational details (contact numbers, frequencies, limits) as needing current verification.
- Confirm the question is about traffic policing/safety rather than live/current road-condition status better sourced from NHMP's current official channels.

## Common Pitfalls

- Describing NHMP as covering all Pakistani roads rather than specifically motorways and national highways.
- Confusing NHMP (traffic enforcement) with NHA (road-infrastructure construction and maintenance).
- Treating NHMP as having jurisdiction over city/urban traffic, which actually falls to separate provincial/city traffic police forces.
- Presenting a specific operational detail (helpline number, FM frequency) as certainly current without flagging it for verification.
