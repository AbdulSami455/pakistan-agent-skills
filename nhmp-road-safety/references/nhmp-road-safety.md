# NHMP Reference

## Source

- National Highways & Motorway Police (NHMP), Pakistan
- NHMP official website: `https://nhmp.gov.pk`

## Jurisdiction and History

- Formed in 1997 as the Pakistan Motorways Police to police the newly built motorway network, beginning with the M-2.
- Expanded in 2001 to also patrol national highways, beginning with the N-5 (Pakistan's longest national highway), leading to the renamed "National Highways & Motorway Police" (NHMP) mandate.
- Jurisdiction is specifically the motorway and national highway network — not city/urban roads, which fall under separate provincial/city traffic police forces (e.g., Islamabad Traffic Police).

## Functions and Services

- Traffic and safety law enforcement, including speed-limit enforcement via speed cameras.
- Accident response, recovery, and security patrolling on the motorway/highway network.
- 24-hour nationwide helpline (commonly cited as 130) accessible from mobile and landline phones.
- FM-95 radio service broadcasting road-safety information and traffic advisories.

## Distinction from Related Bodies

- National Highway Authority (NHA): responsible for planning, building, and maintaining national highway/motorway physical infrastructure — a construction/maintenance body, distinct from NHMP's law-enforcement role.
- Provincial/city traffic police: handle urban traffic enforcement within city limits, separate from NHMP's motorway/highway-specific jurisdiction.

## Extraction Notes

- Always clarify NHMP's jurisdiction is motorways/national highways specifically, not all roads.
- Keep NHMP (policing) distinct from NHA (infrastructure) despite the similar naming.
- Treat specific operational details (helpline number, FM frequency, speed limits) as needing confirmation against NHMP's current official information.
