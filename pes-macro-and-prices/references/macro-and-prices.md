# Macro and Prices Reference

## Source

- Pakistan Economic Survey 2025-26 PDF
- Official source provided by user: `https://www.finance.gov.pk/survey/chapter_26/Complete_PES2025_26.pdf`

## Chapter Map

- Overview of the Economy: contents pages `i-xv`
- Growth and Investment: survey page `3`
- Inflation: survey page `119`

## Section Entry Points

- Growth and Investment opens with global and domestic macro framing around survey pages `3-6`.
- Look for `1.2-a Aggregate Demand in FY 2026` around survey page `6` when the question is about demand-side composition.
- Inflation begins around survey page `119`.
- Use `7.1 Consumer Price Index (National)` around survey page `120` for CPI-focused questions.
- Use `7.2 Wholesale Price Index (WPI) Inflation` around survey page `125` when the question is not about CPI.
- Use `7.3 Sensitive Price Indicator (SPI) inflation` around survey page `126` for short-horizon retail price pressure.
- Use `7.4 Trend of Global Prices`, `7.5 FAO Food Price Index`, and `7.6 Price Control Measures` around survey pages `128-129` for external price drivers and administrative response.

## Indicator Guide

- real GDP growth
- agriculture, industry, and services growth
- investment conditions and confidence language
- aggregate demand composition
- average CPI inflation
- month-specific CPI inflation where stated
- WPI inflation
- SPI inflation
- policy backdrop affecting prices and stabilization

## Extraction Notes

- Preserve exact periods such as `July-April FY 2026`.
- Separate provisional GDP estimates from final or comparative claims.
- Inflation discussion may include both annual averages and individual months.
- When the survey attributes price changes to supply, exchange-rate, or geopolitical factors, keep the attribution close to the source wording.
- If the question asks for `inflation`, verify whether it means average CPI, year-on-year CPI, WPI, or SPI before answering.
- If the question asks for `growth`, verify whether it means headline GDP, sector growth, or demand-side performance.
