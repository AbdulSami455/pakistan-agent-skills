# PES Macro and Prices

## Overview

Use this skill for Pakistan Economic Survey 2025-26 questions about the macroeconomic overview, growth performance, investment conditions, and inflation. This skill is the right entry point when the task asks for headline economic performance, broad stabilization trends, or price developments.

## Chapter Coverage

- Overview of the Economy: contents pages `i-xv`
- Chapter 1: Growth and Investment: starts at survey page `3`
- Chapter 7: Inflation: starts at survey page `119`

## Use This Skill For

- extracting headline GDP growth for FY 2026
- comparing sector growth across agriculture, industry, and services
- summarizing macro stabilization themes
- extracting inflation levels, CPI trends, or price-pressure narratives
- comparing current-year macro outcomes with the previous year

## Routing Rules

- Use the overview section for cross-chapter headline claims.
- Use Chapter 1 when the question is about GDP growth, investment, global backdrop, or sectoral composition of growth.
- Use Chapter 7 when the question is specifically about inflation, CPI, SPI, price drivers, or base effects.
- If a question crosses macro and fiscal themes, start here for the headline context and then read the fiscal skill if the answer needs budget detail.

## Extraction Workflow

1. Identify whether the question is about macro overview, growth, investment, or inflation.
2. Read the smallest relevant section instead of scanning the full survey.
3. Preserve exact year labels such as `FY 2025`, `FY 2026`, `July-April FY 2026`, or `Q1-FY 2026`.
4. Distinguish provisional, estimated, projected, and realized values.
5. Quote figures with units exactly as presented: percent, basis points, US dollars, or rupees.
6. If multiple values appear for similar indicators, explain the period difference instead of collapsing them into one number.

## Technical Rules

- Do not mix survey page numbering with PDF page numbering without making that distinction explicit.
- Treat the overview chapter as synthesis, not as a replacement for chapter-specific detail.
- When inflation is described as moderate, manageable, or rising, pair the narrative with the exact CPI period and value.
- When citing GDP growth, preserve whether the figure is provisional or final.
- Keep sector-growth comparisons aligned to the same period basis.

## Validation Checklist

- Confirm whether the number belongs to FY 2026, FY 2025, or a sub-period such as July-April.
- Check if the reported inflation figure is average inflation, year-on-year inflation, or a specific month.
- Verify whether a macro claim in the overview is repeated with different detail in Chapter 1 or Chapter 7.
- Avoid converting narrative claims into stronger causal statements than the survey makes.

## Common Pitfalls

- Confusing headline GDP growth with sector-level growth rates.
- Mixing average CPI inflation with month-specific CPI inflation.
- Missing the low-base effect discussion when comparing inflation across years.
- Treating the overview as the sole source when a chapter contains the exact table.
