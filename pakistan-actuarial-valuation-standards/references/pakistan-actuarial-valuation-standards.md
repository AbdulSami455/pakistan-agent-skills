## Document Structure

- The Insurance Ordinance, 2000 sets the statutory backbone: Section 34 addresses reserving for non-life (general) insurance business; Sections 35 and 50 address minimum actuarial reserves/policyholder liabilities for life insurance business.
- SECP's Actuarial Valuation Rules (as modernized in the 2025 consultation round) are described as extending this statutory backbone with: gross premium valuation (GPV) methodology for long-term insurance and family takaful business; updated premium and claims reserving methodology for short-term (non-life/general) insurance and takaful business; principle-based and internally consistent actuarial assumption-setting; data-quality requirements; and standardized actuarial submission formats to SECP.
- Separately, SECP has directed all insurance companies to establish an "Actuarial Function" — an internal governance function, scaled to the nature/size/complexity of the insurer's business, responsible for reserve adequacy, solvency-margin input, and assumption governance, reporting per the Insurance Ordinance's actuarial requirements.
- PSOA's website documents the professional-body structure: membership grades, qualification pathway (often coordinated with international actuarial bodies given Pakistan does not yet have a fully independent local qualification examination system equivalent to SOA/IFoA), and its consultative role with SECP on regulatory actuarial standards.

## Key Concepts

- **Gross premium valuation (GPV)**: an actuarial reserving method for long-term (life/family takaful) liabilities that explicitly projects future premiums, benefits, and expenses using best-estimate (or prescribed prudent) assumptions, discounting the net cash flows — contrasted with simpler net premium valuation approaches that do not explicitly reserve for expenses/lapses in the same way.
- **Premium and claims reserving (short-term business)**: for non-life/general insurance and general takaful, reserving typically involves unearned premium reserves (UPR) for unexpired risk and claims reserves (reported and IBNR — incurred but not reported) for outstanding liabilities.
- **Actuarial Function**: SECP's mandated internal governance function at each insurer, responsible for ensuring reserve/solvency adequacy and assumption discipline; distinct from the external statutory auditor, though both interact around the same financial statements.
- **Appointed Actuary**: in many jurisdictions (and typically expected in Pakistan's framework), a qualified actuary formally designated to sign off on statutory valuations and solvency certifications for an insurer — verify the specific designation/signing-actuary requirement against the current Actuarial Valuation Rules text.
- **IAS 19 (Employee Benefits)**: as adopted in Pakistan via SECP notification under the Companies Act, 2017, IAS 19 governs recognition and measurement of defined-benefit obligations (e.g., gratuity, pension) for corporate employers. Key actuarial assumptions include the discount rate (commonly referenced to Pakistan Investment Bond, PIB, yields matching the liability duration as of the valuation date), expected salary growth, withdrawal/turnover rates, and mortality tables (often adapted international tables in the absence of a fully Pakistan-specific standard mortality table, subject to actuarial judgment).
- **Projected Unit Credit Method**: the actuarial cost method IAS 19 requires for defined-benefit obligations, allocating benefit cost to periods of service and revaluing the liability at each reporting date using current assumptions.
- **Service cost vs. net interest vs. remeasurement**: IAS 19 requires current and past service cost and net interest to be recognized in profit or loss, while actuarial gains/losses (remeasurements) on the defined-benefit obligation and plan assets are recognized in other comprehensive income (OCI), not recycled to profit or loss in later periods.

## Extraction Notes

- Always distinguish the insurer/takaful statutory-reserving track (SECP/Insurance Ordinance) from the employer IAS 19 employee-benefit track — they use related actuarial techniques (discounting, mortality/withdrawal assumptions) but serve entirely different purposes and are governed by different frameworks.
- Treat any cited numeric assumption (discount rate, mortality table, solvency margin percentage) as illustrative unless sourced to a specific, dated valuation report or the current regulatory text — these are actuarial judgments/regulatory parameters that change over time and by engagement.
- Flag clearly that neither this skill nor a general-purpose answer substitutes for a qualified actuary's (PSOA-affiliated or internationally designated) engagement-specific valuation, especially for statutory filings or financial-statement figures with real audit/regulatory consequences.
- Cross-reference `secp-insurance-industry-statistics` for aggregate/company-level premium and asset data, and `secp-financial-reporting-standards` for the broader IFRS-adoption and disclosure framework within which IAS 19 sits.
