## Structure Notes

- Freight traffic: tonnage carried, ton-kilometers, by commodity group

- Financial performance: revenue, expenses, subsidy, profit/loss
- Infrastructure: track length, stations, electrification status

## Named Mainlines/Routes

- **Main Line-1 (ML-1)**: Pakistan Railways' primary and busiest trunk route, running from Karachi through Hyderabad, Multan, Lahore, and Rawalpindi to Peshawar. It carries the bulk of both passenger and freight traffic on the network and is the backbone route underlying most system-wide traffic and revenue statistics.
- **ML-1 Upgradation Project**: a long-planned, publicly documented project to rebuild/upgrade the ML-1 track, signaling, and rolling stock, widely discussed as a flagship project under the China–Pakistan Economic Corridor (CPEC) framework. The project has seen repeated timeline revisions, phased-implementation discussion, and financing negotiation delays over the years. Confirm current project status, financing terms, and phase/completion timeline from a current source before citing progress, cost, or dates (verify) — these details have shifted multiple times since the project was first announced.
- Other named lines appear in Yearbook route/section tables (e.g., additional main lines and branch lines connecting Sindh, Punjab, and Balochistan); treat the Yearbook's own route/section naming and alignment for a given edition as authoritative rather than assuming a numbering convention from other sources (verify specific route numbering/alignment beyond ML-1 if precision is required).

## Financial Position — Subsidy Dependency

- Pakistan Railways has a well-documented, long-standing status as a chronically loss-making, subsidy-dependent state-owned enterprise: its operating revenue has for many years fallen short of its operating expenses, requiring recurring government (federal budget) subsidy/grant-in-aid to remain operational and to meet salary and pension obligations. This is a specific, citable structural fact about the organization's finances, not merely a generic "financial challenges" statement. When describing Pakistan Railways' financial performance, state explicitly whether a quoted revenue/profit figure is before or after subsidy, since the pre-subsidy operating position has historically been negative in most recent fiscal years. Exact annual subsidy amounts and loss figures should be sourced from the specific Yearbook edition or Ministry of Finance/public-sector-enterprise disclosures rather than assumed constant year to year (verify current-year figures).

## Extraction Notes

- Always anchor a figure to its fiscal year.
- Distinguish raw counts (passengers, tons) from distance-weighted measures (passenger-km, ton-km).
- Note whether financial performance figures are stated gross or net of government subsidy.
