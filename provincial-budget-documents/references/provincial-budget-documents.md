# Provincial Budget Documents Reference

## Source

- Provincial Finance Departments: Punjab Finance Department, Sindh Finance Department, Khyber Pakhtunkhwa Finance Department, and Balochistan Finance Department, each publishing its own annual budget set on its respective government website.
- Punjab Finance Department: `https://finance.punjab.gov.pk`
- Sindh Finance Department: `https://sindhfinance.gov.pk`
- Khyber Pakhtunkhwa Finance Department: `https://kpfinance.gov.pk`
- Balochistan Finance Department: `https://balochistan.gov.pk` (Finance section)

## Document Map (typical across provinces)

- White Paper / Budget Speech: narrative summary of the province's budget priorities, headline allocations, and policy measures for the upcoming fiscal year.
- Annual Budget Statement: the consolidated statement of estimated receipts and expenditure, current and development, required for legislative approval.
- Schedule of New Expenditure: itemized new spending proposals not previously authorized.
- Annual Development Programme (ADP): scheme-wise listing of development projects and their allocations, usually organized by sector (e.g. health, education, irrigation, roads).
- Own-receipts annex: provincial tax revenue (e.g. provincial sales tax on services, agriculture income tax, property tax, motor vehicle tax) and non-tax receipts, reported separately from the federal NFC transfer.

## Indicator Guide

- total provincial budget size (current + development)
- ADP size and sector/scheme-wise development allocations
- own-receipts (provincial tax and non-tax revenue) and their composition
- federal transfers (NFC award share) received by the province
- department-wise current expenditure allocations

## Extraction Notes

- Each province's Finance Department uses its own document naming and formatting conventions; do not assume Punjab's structure applies identically to Sindh, KP, or Balochistan.
- ADP tables are typically the most granular source for development scheme-level detail; the Annual Budget Statement usually shows only sector-level development totals.
- Own-receipts must be read separately from federal NFC transfers; provincial "total resources" figures combine both, and conflating them misstates a province's fiscal self-reliance.
- Treat budget estimates, revised estimates, and actuals as distinct columns within the same table; provincial budget books commonly present all three for comparison.
