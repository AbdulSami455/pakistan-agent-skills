# Pakistan UNESCO Heritage Sites

## Overview

Use this skill as a quick reference for Pakistan's UNESCO World Heritage Sites and Tentative List entries. This is a small, mostly static reference dataset rather than a document-extraction or data-workflow skill — there is no large corpus to parse and no live indicator series to track. Treat it as a lookup table.

## Inscribed World Heritage Sites (6)

- **Archaeological Ruins at Moenjodaro** — Sindh; inscribed 1980; Indus Valley Civilization urban site.
- **Buddhist Ruins of Takht-i-Bahi and Neighbouring City Remains at Sahr-i-Bahlol** — Khyber Pakhtunkhwa; inscribed 1980.
- **Taxila** — Punjab; inscribed 1980; Gandharan Buddhist archaeological complex spanning multiple sites.
- **Historical Monuments at Makli, Thatta** — Sindh; inscribed 1981; necropolis with monumental tombs.
- **Fort and Shalamar Gardens in Lahore** — Punjab; inscribed 1981 (Lahore Fort and Shalamar Gardens are a single combined inscription).
- **Rohtas Fort** — Punjab; inscribed 1992; Sher Shah Suri-era fortification.

Note: counts and exact inscription years should be re-verified against the UNESCO World Heritage Centre's official list if precision is required, since this skill is a static reference snapshot and UNESCO listings are the authoritative source of record.

## Tentative List

Pakistan also maintains a Tentative List of sites nominated for future inscription. This list changes over time as the government adds or updates submissions, so treat any specific Tentative List entry as subject to change and verify against the UNESCO Tentative List page for Pakistan before treating it as current. Historically the list has included sites such as ancient/historic urban centers, forts, shrines, and natural sites across multiple provinces — do not assume a fixed, permanent set.

## Use This Skill For

- identifying which sites in Pakistan are inscribed UNESCO World Heritage Sites
- providing location (province), inscription year, and brief description for an inscribed site
- distinguishing inscribed sites from Tentative List sites
- general cultural-heritage reference questions about Pakistan

## Use This Skill For — Not

- Detailed conservation status, threat assessments, or management-plan analysis (not covered by this lightweight reference)
- Tourism logistics or visitor information
- Treating this skill's Tentative List snapshot as current without verification — always flag Tentative List entries as potentially outdated

## Validation Checklist

- Confirm a site is "inscribed" (on the official World Heritage List) versus merely "Tentative List" before stating it as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
- Verify inscription year and criteria against the UNESCO World Heritage Centre if the question requires precision (e.g., for a citation).
- Note that "Lahore Fort and Shalamar Gardens" is one combined inscription, not two separate sites, to avoid double-counting Pakistan's site total.

## Common Pitfalls

- Counting Lahore Fort and Shalamar Gardens as two separate World Heritage Sites instead of one combined inscription.
- Treating a Tentative List site as if it were already inscribed.
- Citing an inscription year without checking it against the current UNESCO record, since this skill is a static snapshot that can drift out of date.
