# SBP ICT Exports Special Section Reference

## Source

- State Bank of Pakistan (SBP): `https://www.sbp.org.pk`
- Appears as a "Special Section" or feature box within SBP's Annual Report, Half-Year Report, or Quarterly Report; presence is not guaranteed in every edition.

## Structure Notes

- Definition/scope of IT/ICT exports under balance-of-payments classification
- Export value trend over recent years
- Sub-segment breakdown (software exports vs. IT-enabled services/BPO)
- Destination-market analysis
- Discussion of formal banked receipts versus freelancer/informal inflows
- Policy discussion: tax incentives, export facilitation, exchange retention schemes for IT exporters
- Occasional peer-country benchmarking

## Distinguishing From Other Sources

- `sbp-annual-report`: covers the full SBP annual report narrative; this skill is only the IT/ICT-focused special section within it (or within the half-year/quarterly report).
- `pes-infrastructure-digital-and-climate`: PES's IT/telecom chapter is a brief, survey-level narrative without SBP's balance-of-payments analytical depth.
- `i2i-startup-ecosystem-report`: covers venture funding and ecosystem players, not export earnings.
- **PSEB (Pakistan Software Export Board)**: a Ministry of IT & Telecommunication body that promotes/tracks the IT industry (company registration, facilitation, and sometimes its own industry-size or export-potential estimates). PSEB data is not sourced or covered by this skill and is not methodologically interchangeable with SBP's banked-receipts BOP figures — flag the source explicitly whenever an "IT exports" figure is cited, since SBP and PSEB numbers frequently diverge and get conflated in media reporting.
- `pta-telecom-indicators`: covers the telecom sector specifically (operators, teledensity, broadband/mobile subscriber counts, telecom revenue) — a different industry and dataset from IT/ITeS software and services export receipts. Do not use telecom-sector growth as a stand-in for IT export performance.

## Why This Distinction Matters

"IT exports"/"ICT exports" headline figures in Pakistani public discourse can originate from at least three non-interchangeable sources: SBP's BOP-based banked export receipts (this skill), PSEB's industry-tracking estimates, and PBS/commerce-ministry trade-in-services classifications. These differ because of what is counted (formal banking channel only vs. broader industry estimates that may include undocumented/informal channels) and differing methodology/timing. Always state which source a figure comes from rather than presenting a single "IT exports" number as if it were undisputed across sources.

## Extraction Notes

- Always confirm which specific SBP report and period contains the special section before quoting it; not all editions include one.
- Preserve the BOP-based classification scope SBP uses, since it can differ from commerce-ministry or PBS trade classifications.
