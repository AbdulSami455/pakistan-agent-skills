# Pakistan Languages Reference

## Source

- Pakistan Bureau of Statistics (PBS), census-based mother-tongue/language reporting (most recently from the 7th Population & Housing Census, 2023)
- PBS official website: `https://www.pbs.gov.pk`
- Census archive: `https://www.pbs.gov.pk/censusarchive/`
- Pakistan Academy of Letters (PAL) — national institution for Pakistani-language literature: `https://pal.gov.pk`

## National Mother-Tongue Shares (2023 census, approximate)

- Punjabi: largest mother-tongue group (~37% nationally).
- Pashto: second-largest (~18%).
- Sindhi: (~14%).
- Saraiki: (~12%).
- Urdu: mother tongue of a comparatively small share (~7%) despite being the national language/lingua franca understood by a large majority of the population.
- Balochi and other languages make up the remainder, alongside dozens of smaller indigenous languages.

## Provincial Linguistic Profiles

- Punjab: predominantly Punjabi-speaking, with a significant Saraiki-speaking minority in southern Punjab.
- Sindh: split between Sindhi speakers and a large Urdu-speaking (largely urban, Karachi-concentrated) population.
- Khyber Pakhtunkhwa: predominantly Pashto-speaking, with Hindko and other minority languages in specific districts.
- Balochistan: linguistically diverse, with Balochi and Pashto as the largest groups, alongside Brahui and other languages.

## Linguistic Diversity

- Pakistan has dozens of indigenous languages (commonly cited estimates range in the 70-80+ range across sources) from multiple language families: Indo-Aryan/Indo-European (Punjabi, Urdu, Saraiki, Hindko), Iranian (Pashto, Balochi), Dravidian (Brahui), and Dardic (Khowar, Shina, Kalasha), among others.
- Minority/regional languages include Balti, Kashmiri, Wakhi, Burushaski, and others concentrated in Gilgit-Baltistan, Azad Jammu & Kashmir, and northern regions.

## Extraction Notes

- Always tie a language-share figure to its specific census round/year.
- Do not conflate "national language" (Urdu's official/lingua-franca status) with "most common mother tongue" (Punjabi, per census data).
- Treat mother-tongue classification as self-reported census data, distinct from ethnicity.
- Cross-reference `pakistan-census-bulletins` for the underlying total population/household figures from the same census round.
