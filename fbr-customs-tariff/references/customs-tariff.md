# Customs Tariff Reference

## Source

- Federal Board of Revenue, Pakistan Customs Tariff: `https://www.fbr.gov.pk` under Customs / Pakistan Customs Tariff.
- Updated annually with the Finance Act, alongside SRO notifications issued throughout the year for specific concessions.

## Schedule Structure

- First Schedule: the main tariff schedule, organized by HS Section, Chapter (2-digit), Heading (4-digit), Subheading (6-digit), and national tariff line (8-digit), with the standard customs duty rate for each line.
- Fifth Schedule: concessionary rates for specified goods, often tied to sector (e.g. machinery, pharmaceuticals, agriculture inputs) or end-use conditions.
- SRO notifications: statutory regulatory orders that grant, amend, or withdraw concessions outside the main schedule, frequently issued between budgets.
- Other duty layers referenced alongside customs duty: regulatory duty (RD), additional customs duty (ACD), and anti-dumping/countervailing duties where applicable.

## Indicator Guide

- HS code (2/4/6/8-digit) and its associated customs duty rate
- applicable Fifth Schedule concession and its conditions
- regulatory duty (RD) and additional customs duty (ACD) layered on specific lines
- year-over-year tariff line changes introduced with the Finance Act

## Extraction Notes

- Always work at the most granular HS code level available (8-digit national tariff line) since rates can differ between codes that appear similar at the heading level.
- A "tariff change" in budget commentary may refer to the base customs duty, RD, ACD, or a Fifth Schedule concession; verify which layer changed before describing the net effect.
- SROs can amend tariff treatment outside the annual budget cycle; if the question concerns a specific date, check whether an SRO altered the schedule before or after that date.
- When unsure of the correct HS chapter for a product, narrow down by the product's primary material or function first, then confirm against the chapter notes in the tariff schedule.
