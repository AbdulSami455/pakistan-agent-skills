# PEC Structural Engineering Standards

## Overview

Use this skill for the Pakistan Engineering Council's (PEC) role as the statutory regulator of the engineering profession: engineer and firm registration, the Construction & Operation of Engineering Works Bye-Laws, engineering-work categories, and professional-practice enforcement. This is the right skill when the question is "who is legally allowed to design/sign off on this engineering work and under what registration category," not "what does the seismic code technically require" (that is `bcp-seismic-design-provisions`).

## Coverage

- PEC is a statutory body constituted under the Pakistan Engineering Council Act, 1976 (amended, most recently reflected up to 1 December 2016 in consolidated versions), responsible for regulating the engineering profession in Pakistan.
- Individual engineer registration: PEC registers engineers (typically after a recognized engineering degree and requisite experience) into categories/grades that determine what scope and scale of engineering work they may legally undertake and sign off on.
- Firm/consultancy registration: engineering consulting firms and construction firms must separately register with PEC (as distinct from individual engineer registration) to be eligible to bid on or execute public-sector engineering works, categorized by discipline and financial/technical capacity.
- Construction & Operation of Engineering Works Bye-Laws (originally 1987, subject to amendment) set out the categories of "engineering works" (e.g., structural categories including buildings, bridge structures) and the registration/experience prerequisites tied to each category — this bye-law structure is the mechanism that links a named engineer's registration to the class of structural work they may legally undertake.
- PEC Registration Policy (periodically updated — a 2017 policy has been reissued/updated in subsequent years, e.g., a July 2024 update) sets out current eligibility criteria, application procedure, and category definitions for both engineers and firms.
- PEC also plays a convening role in national engineering-code development (e.g., it convened the task force that produced BCP-SP 2007) and holds a broader engineering-standards mandate distinct from any single code.

## Use This Skill For

- explaining PEC's statutory role and how engineer/firm registration works
- describing engineering-work categories (e.g., structural/civil categories such as buildings vs. bridge structures) and which registration grade is required for a given scale/type of project
- clarifying the difference between individual engineer registration and firm/consultancy registration
- explaining PEC's role in convening or endorsing national engineering codes (e.g., BCP-SP 2007) as a process fact, distinct from the code's technical content
- identifying the governing legal instrument (PEC Act 1976, Bye-Laws, Registration Policy) behind a professional-practice question

## When Not to Use This Skill

- For the technical content of the seismic design code itself (response spectrum methodology, code scope, structural provisions), use `bcp-seismic-design-provisions` instead.
- For a different professional body's registration/licensing regime (e.g., HEC accreditation of engineering degree programs), check whether `hec-accreditation-councils` is the better fit — PEC registers practicing engineers and firms; HEC accredits academic programs, and the two are related but distinct gatekeeping layers.
- For dam, highway, telecom, or power-sector-specific technical standards, use the relevant specialized skill (`pakistan-dam-safety-engineering`, `nha-highway-pavement-bridge-standards`, `pta-spectrum-engineering-allocation`, `nepra-grid-interconnection-code`) rather than this skill; PEC is the cross-cutting professional regulator, not the sector-specific technical-standard-setter for any one of these.

## Routing Rules

- Use this skill when the question is about who may legally practice, register, or sign off on engineering work, or about PEC's bylaws/registration mechanics.
- Use `bcp-seismic-design-provisions` when the question is about what a specific design code technically requires, even though PEC convened the task force that produced it.
- If a question mixes "am I allowed to design this" (registration/regulatory question) with "how do I design this" (technical-code question), separate the two and route each portion to the correct skill.
- If the question concerns program accreditation (a university's engineering degree being PEC-recognized for registration eligibility) rather than an individual's post-graduation registration, note that accreditation and registration are distinct steps in the pipeline and check `hec-accreditation-councils` for the academic-accreditation layer if needed.

## Extraction Workflow

1. Identify whether the question concerns individual engineer registration, firm/consultancy registration, or a specific engineering-work category's requirements.
2. Identify the governing instrument: PEC Act 1976 (statutory basis), the Construction & Operation of Engineering Works Bye-Laws (category/scope definitions), or the current Registration Policy (procedural/eligibility detail) — these serve different functions and should not be treated as one interchangeable document.
3. When citing a specific registration category, grade, or eligibility threshold, note that these are periodically revised (the Registration Policy has been reissued/updated multiple times, e.g., 2017 and a July 2024 update) and should be confirmed against the current PEC-published policy rather than an older cached version.
4. Keep PEC's professional-regulatory role distinct from its historical role convening the BCP-SP 2007 task force; the latter is a one-time process fact, not an ongoing regulatory function comparable to registration.
5. For firm registration questions relevant to public-sector bidding, note that PEC firm registration is typically a precondition layered alongside (not a replacement for) other regulatory requirements such as tax/SECP registration.

## Technical Rules

- Individual engineer registration and firm/consultancy registration are distinct PEC processes with different criteria; do not conflate them.
- Engineering-work categories (e.g., structural categories such as buildings versus bridge structures) determine the scope of work a registered engineer or firm may undertake; do not assume a general "structural engineer" registration authorizes every structural work category without checking the specific category.
- The PEC Act, the Bye-Laws, and the Registration Policy are layered instruments (statute, subordinate regulation, and current procedural policy respectively); cite the correct layer for the type of question asked.
- Registration policy details (eligibility criteria, category names, experience thresholds) change over time across policy updates; do not assume a remembered detail is still current without noting it should be verified against PEC's latest published policy.
- This skill is not a substitute for a licensed, PEC-registered engineer's actual registration status check or professional judgment on an active project.

## Validation Checklist

- Confirm whether the question is about individual registration, firm registration, or a specific work category's requirements.
- Confirm which governing instrument (Act, Bye-Laws, Registration Policy) is relevant and note its approximate vintage/update status.
- Verify that any cited registration category or eligibility threshold is checked against PEC's current published policy rather than assumed from memory.
- Confirm whether the question actually needs PEC (professional regulation) or a technical design-code skill, and route accordingly if it is the latter.

## Common Pitfalls

- Treating PEC's technical code-convening role (e.g., for BCP-SP 2007) as equivalent to its ongoing professional-registration regulatory function.
- Conflating individual engineer registration with firm/consultancy registration.
- Citing a specific registration category, grade, or threshold without flagging that PEC registration policy is periodically updated and should be verified against the current version.
- Confusing PEC's registration mandate with HEC's academic-accreditation mandate — they are sequential but distinct gatekeeping steps in an engineer's professional pipeline.
