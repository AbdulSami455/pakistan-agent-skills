# PEMRA Reference

## Source

- Pakistan Electronic Media Regulatory Authority (PEMRA), established 2002
- PEMRA official website: `https://pemra.gov.pk`
- Regulatory framework documents and consultation papers (e.g., on regulating web TV/OTT) are published under PEMRA's publications/uploads sections.

## Licensing Categories

- Satellite TV: national/international-scale broadcast licenses.
- Regional/provincial-scale TV licenses, limited to specific geographic coverage.
- FM radio licenses.
- Cable TV distribution licenses (local cable operators).
- Satellite/DTH (direct-to-home) distribution licenses.
- Web TV/OTT content platform rules — a more recently developed regulatory area extending PEMRA's traditional broadcast mandate to internet-delivered content.

## Regulatory Structure

- Licensing follows an open, competitive bidding process when applications exceed available slots in a category, alongside standard eligibility/ownership requirements.
- Content is governed by PEMRA's Code of Conduct and related rules; violations can trigger a notice/show-cause process, fines, content bans, or license suspension/cancellation.
- PEMRA's licensing rules also intersect with cross-media ownership concentration limits.

## Extraction Notes

- Always identify the specific platform/license category before describing licensing rules, since requirements differ by category.
- Treat any specific channel's current license/enforcement status as time-sensitive; direct to PEMRA's official notifications for confirmation.
- Distinguish PEMRA's broadcast/electronic-media mandate from PTA's telecom-infrastructure mandate — see `pta-telecom-indicators` for the latter.
- Note that OTT/web-TV regulation is a newer, still-evolving policy area relative to PEMRA's established broadcast/cable framework.
