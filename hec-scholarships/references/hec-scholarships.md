# HEC Scholarships Reference

## Source

- Higher Education Commission (HEC) of Pakistan
- HEC official website: `https://www.hec.gov.pk`
- Scholarships hub: `https://www.hec.gov.pk/site/scholarships`
- Scholarship Portal (application/tracking): `https://scholarship.hec.gov.pk`
- Need-Based Scholarships program page: `https://www.hec.gov.pk/english/scholarshipsgrants/NBS/Pages/default.aspx`
- Provincial HEC scholarship pages (e.g., Sindh HEC) are separately administered — confirm the correct provincial site when relevant.

## Programme Types

- Need-Based Scholarships (NBS): domestic, income-threshold-based, for public-sector university students.
- Donor-funded merit-and-needs-based programs (e.g., USAID-supported schemes), often institution-specific.
- Overseas PhD scholarships (e.g., country-specific phases funding PhD study abroad).
- Jointly administered international schemes (e.g., Commonwealth Scholarships facilitated with partner-country agencies).

## Key Indicator Types

- eligibility criteria: domicile, household income threshold, academic merit (CGPA/percentage), discipline restrictions
- coverage: tuition-only, tuition+stipend, or full funding (tuition, stipend, travel, settling-in allowance)
- application cycle: opening/closing dates, required documents, selection process stages
- participating institutions: some schemes restrict eligibility to a specific list of universities

## Extraction Notes

- Always identify the specific scheme by name; HEC runs multiple concurrent, distinctly structured scholarship programs.
- Confirm whether a cited income threshold, deadline, or participating-institution list reflects the current cycle, since these are revised periodically.
- Distinguish federal HEC administration from provincial HEC administration (e.g., Sindh HEC) even for similarly named schemes.
- Cross-reference `pakistan-university-admissions` only for admission merit/entry-test questions — scholarship eligibility is a separate process from admission.
