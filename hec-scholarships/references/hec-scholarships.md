# HEC Scholarships Reference

## Source

- Higher Education Commission (HEC) of Pakistan
- HEC official website: `https://www.hec.gov.pk`
- Scholarships hub: `https://www.hec.gov.pk/site/scholarships`
- Scholarship Portal (application/tracking): `https://scholarship.hec.gov.pk`
- Need-Based Scholarships program page: `https://www.hec.gov.pk/english/scholarshipsgrants/NBS/Pages/default.aspx`
- Provincial HEC scholarship pages (e.g., Sindh HEC) are separately administered — confirm the correct provincial site when relevant.

## Programme Types

- Need-Based Scholarships (NBS): domestic, income-threshold-based, for public-sector university students. HEC's flagship, longest-running own-branded scheme, targeting undergraduate students unable to afford tuition at HEC-recognized public-sector universities.
- Donor-funded merit-and-needs-based programs (e.g., USAID-supported schemes), often institution-specific.
- Overseas PhD scholarships (e.g., country-specific phases funding PhD study abroad).
- Jointly administered international schemes (e.g., Commonwealth Scholarships facilitated with partner-country agencies).

## Named Schemes Roster (for disambiguation)

This roster separates three categories that are easily conflated: (a) HEC's own schemes, (b) schemes HEC merely administers on behalf of another body, and (c) similarly named but administratively separate provincial schemes.

### HEC's own schemes (HEC-designed, HEC-run)
- **HEC Need-Based Scholarship (NBS) Program** — HEC's own long-running need-based undergraduate scholarship for students at partner public-sector universities, funded from HEC's own budget (with donor support, e.g. World Bank, in earlier phases).
- **HEC Indigenous PhD Fellowship / Indigenous PhD Scholarship Program** — HEC's own domestic PhD scholarship scheme supporting doctoral study at Pakistani universities, run historically in numbered batches/phases, as distinct from HEC's overseas PhD scholarship lines.
- **HEC Overseas Scholarships for PhD** (various country-specific phases, e.g., HEC-DAAD for Germany, HEC-USAID-funded phases, and other partner-country arrangements) — HEC's own overseas PhD funding lines, though several are co-funded/co-administered jointly with a partner country's agency (e.g., DAAD), so "HEC scholarship" does not always mean "entirely HEC-funded."

### Schemes administered *through* HEC but owned by another body
- **Ehsaas Undergraduate Scholarship Program** — a *federal poverty-alleviation* initiative launched under the Ehsaas umbrella (the federal government's poverty-alleviation strategy), disbursed to undergraduate students via HEC and public-sector universities as the delivery channel. Policy ownership and core funding sit with the federal social-protection apparatus, not HEC itself — do not describe it simply as "an HEC scholarship" without noting this distinction. (Administrative ownership/branding of federal poverty-alleviation programs has shifted over time; confirm current status — verify.)

### Similarly named but administratively separate — provincial schemes (NOT HEC)
- **Punjab Educational Endowment Fund (PEEF)** — a *Government of Punjab* scholarship/endowment scheme, entirely separate from HEC in governance, funding, and eligibility rules. Frequently confused with HEC schemes because both are need-based, but PEEF is provincial, not federal/HEC.
- **Sindh HEC and other provincial higher-education commissions' scholarship programs** — provincial HECs may run their own similarly worded scholarship schemes; these are administratively distinct from the federal HEC even though the parent body shares the "HEC" name.
- Other provincial endowment/scholarship funds (e.g., in Balochistan or Khyber Pakhtunkhwa) follow the same pattern of provincial ownership and separate eligibility/application processes — exact current names and operational status of these should be verified rather than assumed (verify).

## Key Indicator Types

- eligibility criteria: domicile, household income threshold, academic merit (CGPA/percentage), discipline restrictions
- coverage: tuition-only, tuition+stipend, or full funding (tuition, stipend, travel, settling-in allowance)
- application cycle: opening/closing dates, required documents, selection process stages
- participating institutions: some schemes restrict eligibility to a specific list of universities

## Extraction Notes

- Always identify the specific scheme by name; HEC runs multiple concurrent, distinctly structured scholarship programs.
- Confirm whether a cited income threshold, deadline, or participating-institution list reflects the current cycle, since these are revised periodically.
- Distinguish federal HEC administration from provincial HEC administration (e.g., Sindh HEC) even for similarly named schemes.
- Cross-reference `pakistan-university-admissions` only for admission merit/entry-test questions — scholarship eligibility is a separate process from admission.
