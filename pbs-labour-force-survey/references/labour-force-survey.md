# Labour Force Survey Reference

## Source

- Pakistan Bureau of Statistics (PBS), "Labour Force Survey" report
- Concepts and definitions follow ILO-aligned labour force framework as adapted by PBS
- PBS official website: `https://www.pbs.gov.pk`
- Labour Force Survey reports: `https://www.pbs.gov.pk/content/labour-force-survey`

## Document Structure

- Concepts and definitions section: defines labour force, employed, unemployed, underemployed, formal sector, and informal sector per the survey's own technical criteria.
- Headline national indicators: labour force participation rate, unemployment rate, employment-to-population ratio, usually presented first as summary tables.
- Disaggregated tables by sex, age group, area (urban/rural), province/region, education level, industry/sector, and employment status (employer, employee, self-employed, unpaid family worker).
- Formal vs. informal sector employment tables, classified using registration/contract/benefits criteria rather than industry type.
- Underemployment tables: time-related underemployment and unemployment duration by education level.
- Some survey rounds include supplementary modules (e.g. child labour, youth employment).

## Key Indicator Types

- labour force participation rate
- unemployment rate and underemployment rate
- employment by industry/sector and employment status
- formal vs. informal sector employment share
- demographic and regional disaggregations (sex, age, education, urban/rural, province)

## Extraction Notes

- Always state the survey round and reference period; LFS rounds are not always strictly annual.
- Keep "unemployed" and "underemployed" as distinct categories; they are not interchangeable.
- Informal sector classification is based on survey-defined criteria (registration, contract, social security), not simply industry type.
- When citing a demographic subgroup's rate, do not present it as the national headline without noting the subgroup scope.
