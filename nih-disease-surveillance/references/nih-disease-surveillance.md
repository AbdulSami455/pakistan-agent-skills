# NIH Disease Surveillance Reference

## Source

- National Institutes of Health (NIH), Islamabad — autonomous body under the Ministry of National Health Services, Regulation and Coordination
- NIH official website: `https://www.nih.org.pk`
- Guidelines section: `https://www.nih.org.pk/guidelines/`

## Document Structure

- Surveillance bulletins: periodic case-count and trend updates for epidemic-prone diseases, often issued by NIH's Field Epidemiology and Disease Surveillance Division or Center for Infectious Diseases Prevention and Control.
- Case definitions: standardized definitions (confirmed/suspected/probable) for classifying communicable/infectious disease cases, used nationally for consistent reporting.
- Outbreak-specific guidelines: prevention and control guidance for specific diseases or events (e.g., dengue, post-monsoon disease risks, viral hemorrhagic fevers).
- Public Health Emergency Preparedness and Response materials, including national action plans issued during major health emergencies.

## Key Indicator Types

- confirmed and suspected case counts by disease and reporting period
- outbreak trend data (rising/falling case counts over successive reporting periods)
- case-definition criteria for specific diseases
- prevention/control recommendations tied to a specific disease or event

## Extraction Notes

- Always state the disease and exact reporting period for any cited case count.
- Distinguish confirmed from suspected cases where the source differentiates them.
- Treat early-outbreak figures as provisional and check for later, revised bulletins before citing a "final" total.
- Cross-reference `who-pakistan-health-statistics` only for internationally modeled/comparable indicators — NIH figures are Pakistan's own domestically reported surveillance data and may not match WHO's estimates exactly.
- Cross-reference `epi-immunization-schedule` only for routine vaccination-schedule questions, which are a distinct function from outbreak surveillance.
