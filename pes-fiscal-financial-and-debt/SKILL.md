# PES Fiscal, Financial, and Debt

## Overview

Use this skill for Pakistan Economic Survey 2025-26 questions about fiscal performance, monetary conditions, capital markets, corporate developments, and public debt. This is the right skill when the task requires budgetary indicators, policy-rate context, debt-service interpretation, or market-finance chapter detail.

## Chapter Coverage

- Chapter 4: Fiscal Development: starts at survey page `63`
- Chapter 5: Money and Credit: starts at survey page `83`
- Chapter 6: Capital Markets & Corporate Sector: starts at survey page `103`
- Chapter 9: Public Debt: starts at survey page `155`

## Use This Skill For

- extracting fiscal deficit or primary surplus figures
- comparing expenditure or revenue components
- summarizing policy-rate changes or liquidity measures
- extracting capital-market indicators such as index or market-cap language
- analyzing debt trends, servicing pressure, or debt sustainability discussion

## Routing Rules

- Use Chapter 4 for fiscal deficits, expenditure mix, revenue performance, and primary balance.
- Use Chapter 5 for policy rate, inflation-risk implications for monetary stance, and liquidity conditions.
- Use Chapter 6 for capital market, corporate sector, or savings-related market metrics.
- Use Chapter 9 for debt stock, servicing burden, debt indicators, and debt-management narrative.

## Extraction Workflow

1. Identify whether the question is fiscal, monetary, markets, or debt-related.
2. Route to the specific chapter first instead of scanning all finance chapters.
3. Preserve whether figures are stated in percent of GDP, rupees, basis points, or dollars.
4. Keep time labels exact, especially where FY 2025 and FY 2026 budget estimates are adjacent.
5. Distinguish between realized performance, budget estimates, and forward-looking measures.

## Technical Rules

- Fiscal tables often mix levels and shares of GDP; do not convert one into the other unless explicitly asked.
- A fall in markup pressure does not automatically imply a fall in all current expenditure components.
- Preserve basis-point language exactly when discussing policy-rate changes.
- Keep market-performance metrics distinct from macro claims; an index increase is not the same as a broad investment conclusion.
- For debt discussion, keep debt stock, debt servicing, and maturity indicators separate.

## Validation Checklist

- Verify whether the figure is a ratio to GDP, a nominal level, or a growth rate.
- Check whether the fiscal number belongs to FY 2025 actuals or FY 2026 budget estimates.
- For monetary questions, confirm if the answer needs the latest rate, cumulative change, or meeting-specific action.
- For debt, distinguish domestic, external, public, and servicing measures before answering.

## Common Pitfalls

- Mixing primary balance with overall fiscal balance.
- Confusing markup payments with the full current expenditure picture.
- Dropping the basis-point unit from monetary policy moves.
- Treating capital-market performance as direct evidence of all corporate-sector conditions.
