# Federal Budget Documents Reference

## Source

- Ministry of Finance, Government of Pakistan, federal budget documents: `https://www.finance.gov.pk/budget`.
- Released together around the June budget announcement, covering the upcoming fiscal year (July-June).

## Document Map

- Budget in Brief: condensed tables of resources, expenditure, and fiscal aggregates (deficit, financing) for the proposed budget, with comparison columns for prior-year budget and revised estimates.
- Budget Speech: delivered by the Finance Minister, covering macro context, policy priorities, and headline new measures (tax relief, tax measures, subsidies, relief packages).
- Finance Bill: the legislative instrument amending tax statutes (Income Tax Ordinance, Sales Tax Act, Customs Act, Federal Excise Act) to implement budget tax proposals.
- Demand for Grants: detailed, ministry-by-ministry and grant-by-grant expenditure requests requiring National Assembly approval, split into current and development expenditure.

## Indicator Guide

- total budgeted revenue (tax and non-tax) for the upcoming fiscal year
- total budgeted expenditure (current and development)
- budgeted fiscal deficit and financing plan
- ministry/department-wise allocations (Demand for Grants)
- specific new tax measures (rate changes, new levies, exemptions) from the Finance Bill

## Extraction Notes

- Budget in Brief tables typically show three columns: prior-year budget estimate, prior-year revised estimate, and new-year budget estimate; do not conflate these.
- The Finance Bill text is the authoritative source for exact tax law wording; the Budget Speech is a policy summary and may simplify or omit detail.
- Demand for Grants documents are long and structured by grant number; locate the specific ministry/grant rather than scanning sequentially.
- Supplementary grants approved mid-year are separate from the original Demand for Grants and should not be merged into the original allocation figure.
