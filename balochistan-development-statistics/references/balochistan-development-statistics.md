# Balochistan Development Statistics Reference

## Source

- Balochistan Bureau of Statistics, Government of Balochistan
- Published annually as "Balochistan Development Statistics" (sometimes also issued in condensed form as "Balochistan in Figures")
- Official website: `https://bos.gob.pk` (Balochistan Bureau of Statistics) — **(verify) this URL is likely incorrect.** Pakistani government domains conventionally end in `.gov.pk`, not `.gob.pk` ("gob" is the country-code convention used by Spanish-speaking governments, e.g. Mexico's `gob.mx`). This looks like either a typo carried over from an unreliable source or a non-official/lookalike domain. Do not cite or link this URL without independently verifying it resolves to the actual Balochistan Bureau of Statistics. Before using it, check: (a) `https://www.pbs.gov.pk` for links to provincial bureaus, or (b) the Government of Balochistan's main portal for a "Bureau of Statistics" or "Planning & Development Department" link, which should resolve under a `.gov.pk` subdomain.
- Publications section: check the Bureau's "Publications" or "Statistical Reports" page for the latest edition — (verify) exact path once the correct root domain is confirmed.

## Administrative Geography

- Balochistan is Pakistan's largest province by land area but least populous, and it has the largest number of districts of any province — over 30 as of recent reorganizations (exact count varies by edition since new districts are periodically carved out; confirm the boundary vintage before comparing years).
- Representative district examples spanning the province's different regions (not exhaustive): **Quetta** (provincial capital), **Gwadar** (coastal, Makran division), **Kalat**, **Khuzdar**, **Sibi**, **Zhob**, **Loralai**, **Chagai** (Balochistan's largest district by area, bordering Afghanistan/Iran), **Panjgur**.
- This large district count combined with a small and unevenly distributed population (Balochistan has Pakistan's lowest population density) is the concrete basis for the "high sampling variance across districts" caveat already noted in the skill: many districts have small survey sample sizes relative to their area, so district-level rates (e.g., literacy, health indicators) can swing significantly between survey rounds without reflecting real underlying change. Flag this explicitly when a user asks to compare small-district figures year-over-year.

## Extraction Notes

- Always anchor a figure to its administrative level (district/division/province) and reference year.
- Condensed "in Figures" booklets are quick-reference summaries; for full time series and district breakdowns, the main Development Statistics volume is the authoritative source.
- Small-district figures may reflect sampling limitations; note uncertainty when district populations are very small.
