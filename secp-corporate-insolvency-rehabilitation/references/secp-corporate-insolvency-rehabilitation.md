## Document Structure — Corporate Rehabilitation Act, 2018

At a conceptual level (verify exact section numbers against the current Act text before citing them in a specific matter):

- Preliminary provisions: short title, commencement, definitions (including who may be an "administrator"/insolvency practitioner, what constitutes "rehabilitation," and jurisdictional court).
- Provisions on eligibility to apply for rehabilitation (company itself, and potentially creditors/other stakeholders subject to conditions).
- Provisions establishing the moratorium/stay upon admission of a rehabilitation application.
- Provisions on the appointment, powers, and duties of the administrator/rehabilitation practitioner.
- Provisions on formulation of the rehabilitation plan, creditor classes, voting/approval mechanics, and court sanction of an approved plan.
- Provisions on the panel of insolvency experts (Section 5 area) from which practitioners may be drawn — SECP maintains and publishes this panel.
- Provisions on conclusion/termination of rehabilitation proceedings (successful plan implementation, conversion to winding-up if rehabilitation fails, etc.).

## Document Structure — Companies Act, 2017, Winding-Up Provisions

- Voluntary winding-up: members' voluntary winding-up (solvent company, declaration of solvency) vs. creditors' voluntary winding-up (where the company cannot declare solvency) — control over the process and creditor involvement differ between the two.
- Compulsory/court winding-up: petition to the relevant court (commonly on grounds including inability to pay debts, among others set out in the Act), appointment of an Official Liquidator, powers of the court and liquidator during the process.
- Provisions on the powers, duties, and reporting obligations of the liquidator (realization of assets, calling of creditor claims, distribution).
- Provisions on dissolution of the company following completion of winding-up.
- Provisions addressing offenses/misconduct connected with the winding-up (e.g., by officers of the company) — note but do not overstate specifics without verification.

## Key Concepts

| Concept | Rehabilitation (CRA 2018) | Winding-Up (Companies Act 2017) |
|---|---|---|
| Objective | Rescue a distressed but viable company as a going concern | Realize assets, pay creditors, dissolve the company |
| Trigger | Application by company (and, subject to conditions, potentially creditors) to the court | Members'/creditors' resolution (voluntary) or court petition (compulsory), commonly on inability-to-pay-debts or similar grounds |
| Key officer | Administrator / insolvency practitioner (drawn from SECP's panel) | Official Liquidator / liquidator |
| Effect on creditor action | Moratorium/stay on enforcement action against the company (scope to be verified against current Act text) | Distribution follows statutory priority; company ceases to exist upon dissolution |
| Outcome | Court-sanctioned rehabilitation plan implemented; company continues | Assets distributed, company dissolved and struck off |

**Do not confuse with the Corporate Restructuring Companies Act, 2016**: that statute creates a distinct vehicle — a licensed "corporate restructuring company" — that can acquire non-performing loans/distressed assets from banks/financial institutions and restructure them. It is a tool aimed at cleaning up financial institutions' balance sheets, not the mechanism by which a distressed operating company itself is rescued (that is CRA 2018's role). The two Acts were part of the same broader legislative push (2016 and 2018) toward a more developed restructuring/insolvency ecosystem in Pakistan but serve different actors and purposes.

## Extraction Notes

- Never state a specific moratorium duration, creditor-voting threshold percentage, or preferential-debt category as settled fact unless verified against the current Act/Regulations text — these are the kind of precise figures most prone to drift or misstatement, and getting them wrong in a professional context has real consequences.
- When discussing "insolvency practitioners" for a current matter, direct the user to SECP's own published panel list (see Source section) rather than naming specific individuals from memory.
- Flag explicitly that CRA 2018's practical case volume has been limited to date and that SECP has run stakeholder consultations (as of the mid-2020s) on strengthening the insolvency and debt-enforcement framework — this signals the regime is still maturing, not a settled, heavily-litigated area.
- This skill and its reference are not a substitute for licensed legal advice on an active rehabilitation or winding-up matter; recommend a qualified corporate/insolvency lawyer and verification against the court's own procedural rules for any live case.
