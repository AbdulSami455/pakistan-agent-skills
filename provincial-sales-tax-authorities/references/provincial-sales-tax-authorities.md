# Provincial Sales Tax Authorities Reference

## Constitutional and Legal Basis

- Prior to the 18th Constitutional Amendment (2010), "tax on services" sat in a legally ambiguous position, with FBR historically collecting some sales tax on services under federal law in the provinces pending provincial capacity.
- The 18th Amendment removed "tax on services" from the Federal Legislative List. This is the constitutional basis commonly cited for treating sales tax on services as a provincial subject, exercised through each province's own legislature and own dedicated revenue authority.
- Each province subsequently enacted its own Sales Tax on Services Act and established its own collecting authority. There is no single national sales-tax-on-services statute; each province's act, schedule of taxable services, and rate structure stands on its own.

## The Four Provincial Authorities

### Sindh Revenue Board (SRB)
- Established under the Sindh Revenue Board Act, 2010.
- Administers the Sindh Sales Tax on Services Act, 2011.
- Sindh is generally recognized as the first province to operationalize its own sales tax on services collection, beginning collection in 2011 — predating Punjab's authority. (Verify exact operational start date against SRB's own published history if precision is required.)
- Governing act and SRB notifications set out Sindh's own schedule of taxable services and applicable rates by service category.

### Punjab Revenue Authority (PRA)
- Established in 2012 under the Punjab Revenue Authority Act, 2012.
- Administers the Punjab Sales Tax on Services Act, 2012.
- PRA publishes its own Second Schedule (or equivalent) enumerating taxable services and rates, revised via the Punjab Finance Act each year.

### Khyber Pakhtunkhwa Revenue Authority (KPRA)
- Established to administer the Khyber Pakhtunkhwa Finance Act, 2013 provisions on sales tax on services (the KP Sales Tax on Services Act framework); exact establishment-year details should be verified against KPRA's own published history.
- Operates KP's own taxable services schedule and rate structure, revised via KP's annual Finance Act.

### Balochistan Revenue Authority (BRA)
- Administers the Balochistan Sales Tax on Services Act, 2015.
- Generally the most recently operationalized of the four authorities and, in practice, has had a smaller revenue base and administrative footprint relative to PRA, SRB, and KPRA — treat any specific comparative collection figures as needing verification against current BRA/provincial finance department publications.

## Rate Structure — General Pattern (verify current figures)

- Each authority sets its own "standard" or general rate for services not otherwise specified, and this rate is revised independently via each province's annual Finance Act — it is not synchronized across provinces.
- In recent years, general standard rates across the four provinces have broadly clustered in a mid-to-high-teens percentage band, but the exact current rate for any one province must be verified against that province's current Finance Act/notification rather than assumed from a prior year or from another province.
- Numerous services carry sector-specific rates that diverge from the general rate — telecommunication services are a commonly cited example of a service category taxed at a materially higher rate than the general services rate in more than one province. IT and IT-enabled services (ITeS) have, in some provinces and time periods, been granted reduced or exempted treatment to support the sector — again, verify current treatment before citing a figure, since these incentives are revised or withdrawn via annual Finance Acts.
- Because rates are set independently and revised annually and asynchronously, the same service category can carry different rates in different provinces at the same point in time, and the same province can change a given sector's rate from one fiscal year to the next.

## Registration and Multi-Province Compliance

- A service provider must register with the provincial authority(ies) covering the province(s) in which it renders taxable services, in addition to any FBR registration/NTN it holds for income tax or goods-related sales tax purposes.
- A business with service operations in more than one province (e.g., a bank, telecom operator, IT firm, courier/logistics company, or consultancy with branch offices in Punjab, Sindh, KP, and/or Balochistan) may need to register separately with PRA, SRB, KPRA, and/or BRA, file periodic returns to each independently, and reconcile place-of-supply treatment under each province's own act.
- A business dealing in both goods and services may simultaneously need FBR sales tax registration (goods) and one or more provincial sales-tax-on-services registrations (services) — these are not substitutes for one another.

## Place-of-Supply / Jurisdiction Complexity

- Each province's Sales Tax on Services Act contains its own rules for determining where a service is treated as "provided" or "rendered" for tax purposes (e.g., origin of the service provider, location of the recipient, place where the service is actually performed, or a combination). These rules are not uniform across the four provinces.
- Cross-province service delivery (e.g., a Punjab-based firm providing a service to a client in Sindh) can create genuine jurisdictional ambiguity, and in some sectors has been the subject of double-taxation disputes or coordination arrangements between provincial authorities. Treat any specific cross-province jurisdiction claim as needing case-specific verification rather than a general rule.
