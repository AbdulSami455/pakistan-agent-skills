# IMF Pakistan Documents Reference

## Source

- International Monetary Fund, Pakistan country page: `https://www.imf.org/en/Countries/PAK`, covering Article IV consultations and program (EFF/SBA) staff reports and press releases.

## Document Map

- Article IV Staff Report: includes staff appraisal, policy discussions, and often a Selected Issues Paper annex on specific topics.
- Program Staff Report (per review): includes program performance review, quantitative performance criteria/indicative targets table, structural benchmarks table, and staff risk assessment.
- Letter of Intent / Memorandum of Economic and Financial Policies (MEFP): government's own commitments, usually annexed to the staff report.
- Executive Board press release: short summary of the Board's decision following a review or new arrangement approval.

## Indicator Guide

- quantitative performance criteria (e.g. primary balance floor, net international reserves floor, net domestic assets ceiling)
- structural benchmarks and their due dates/status
- IMF staff growth, inflation, and fiscal projections for Pakistan
- staff risk assessment language and balance-of-risks characterization
- program disbursement amounts tied to each review

## Extraction Notes

- Each review is numbered (e.g. first, second review) and revises targets going forward; always anchor any cited target to its specific review document.
- Performance criteria are typically tested at specific test dates; structural benchmarks have due dates that may be reset/rephased across reviews — track both carefully.
- IMF staff projections (growth, inflation, fiscal aggregates) are IMF's own and frequently differ from the government's PES figures for the same period; note both where relevant for comparison tasks.
- The MEFP, where annexed, represents the government's stated commitments and can be a useful cross-check against the federal budget documents for the same fiscal year.
