# NTS Reference

## Source

- National Testing Service Pakistan (NTS)
- NTS official website: `https://www.nts.org.pk`
- Test schedules, registration, and advertisements for both standardized tests (NAT/GAT) and custom recruitment tests are published on the official site per cycle/advertisement.

## Test Types

- NAT (National Aptitude Test): undergraduate-level aptitude test, run in multiple categories aligned to academic background (e.g., pre-medical/pre-engineering-adjacent, computer science, commerce, general), conducted multiple times per year.
- GAT (Graduate Assessment Test): graduate/Master's and PhD-level test, with General and Subject-specific variants; often required by HEC or universities for scholarship or program eligibility.
- HAT and other HEC-mandated or institution-specific tests administered by NTS.
- Custom recruitment/screening tests: one-off tests NTS conducts on behalf of specific government departments or organizations for a job advertisement, structurally distinct from the recurring NAT/GAT cycles.

## Key Structural Notes

- Registration is per-cycle (for NAT/GAT) or per-advertisement (for custom recruitment tests), typically via the NTS website, with fees payable through common mobile-wallet or bank channels.
- Roll number slips and result slips are issued per registered candidate per test cycle.
- NTS scores typically feed into a receiving institution's or organization's own composite eligibility/merit formula rather than standing alone as an admission or hiring decision.

## Extraction Notes

- Always specify which test (NAT, GAT-General, GAT-Subject, or a named custom test) a question concerns.
- Treat any specific test date or cycle as needing confirmation against NTS's current official schedule, since cycles are frequent and dates shift.
- Cross-reference `pakistan-university-admissions` for how a specific university uses an NTS-family score (e.g., HAT) within its own merit list — NTS itself does not set admission merit.
