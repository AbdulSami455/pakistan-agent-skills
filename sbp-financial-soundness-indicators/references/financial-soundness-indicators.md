# Financial Soundness Indicators Reference

## Source

- State Bank of Pakistan, Financial Soundness Indicators and Quarterly Compendium: `https://www.sbp.org.pk/ecodata/fsi.asp`.
- Published as structured tables (often Excel) covering system-wide and bank-wise prudential ratios for each quarter.

## Typical Compendium Structure

- Capital adequacy section: CAR, Tier 1 capital ratio, leverage ratio.
- Asset quality section: gross NPLs, net NPLs, NPL ratio, provisioning/coverage ratio.
- Earnings and profitability section: ROA, ROE, net interest margin, cost-to-income ratio.
- Liquidity section: liquid assets to total assets, advances-to-deposits ratio.
- Bank-wise annex: the same indicators broken out by individual bank or banking group.

## Indicator Guide

- capital adequacy ratio (CAR) and Tier 1 ratio
- gross/net NPLs and NPL ratio
- provisioning coverage ratio
- return on assets (ROA), return on equity (ROE)
- advances-to-deposits ratio (ADR)
- liquid assets to total assets

## Extraction Notes

- FSI data is published with a lag relative to the quarter it covers; always confirm the "as of" date rather than assuming it matches the publication date.
- Regulatory minimum CAR requirements have changed over time; when assessing whether a bank or the system is "well capitalized," check the applicable minimum for that period rather than assuming a fixed threshold.
- Bank-wise tables may use bank names or codes inconsistently across releases; match by full bank name when comparing across quarters.
- Profitability ratios may be reported both quarterly and on a cumulative (year-to-date) basis; state which basis is being used.
