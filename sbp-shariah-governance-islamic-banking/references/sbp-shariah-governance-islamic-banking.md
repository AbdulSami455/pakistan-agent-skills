# SBP Shariah Governance and Islamic Banking Reference

## Source

- State Bank of Pakistan, Islamic Banking / Islamic Finance overview: `https://www.sbp.org.pk/our-operations/islamic-banking`
- IFPD Circular No. 08 of 2024 (revised Shariah Governance Framework, effective for compliance by IBIs into 2025): `https://www.sbp.org.pk/ifpd/2024/C8.htm`
- Shariah Governance Framework for Islamic Banking Institutions — Annex to IFPD Circular No. 08 of 2024: `https://www.sbp.org.pk/ifpd/2024/C8-Annex.pdf`
- Shari'ah Governance Framework for Islamic Banking Institutions — earlier (2018) framework annex, for historical/comparative reference: `https://www.sbp.org.pk/ibd/2018/C1-Annex-A.pdf`

## Document Structure

- SBP's Islamic Finance department (IFPD, formerly Islamic Banking Department/IBD) issues the SGF as a circular with a detailed technical annex.
- The SGF annex typically covers: definitions and scope (which entities qualify as IBIs); Shariah Board composition, appointment, and fit-and-proper criteria; the three-lines-of-defence model (Shariah Compliance, Shariah Review, Shariah Audit); Shariah non-compliance risk management and income purification; product approval and Shariah pronouncement (fatwa) process; and reporting/disclosure obligations to SBP.
- SBP has issued successive SGF versions — a foundational framework, a 2015 update, a 2018 revision (see 2018 annex above), and a substantial 2024 revision (IFPD Circular No. 08 of 2024) that IBIs were directed to prepare for by January 1, 2025, with compliance-status submission to SBP required by March 31, 2025. Always identify which version is under discussion.

## Key Concepts

- **Islamic Banking Institution (IBI)**: an SBP-licensed full-fledged Islamic bank, or the Islamic banking branches/window operations of a conventional (commercial) bank operating under a segregated Islamic banking framework.
- **Shariah Board (SB)**: the IBI's apex Shariah authority, composed of qualified Shariah scholars appointed by the Board of Directors; SB rulings on Shariah matters are binding on the institution's Islamic banking operations. Do not treat the SB as merely advisory.
- **Three lines of defence for Shariah compliance**: (1) Shariah Compliance Department — day-to-day monitoring embedded in business operations; (2) Shariah Review function — periodic independent-of-business-line testing of compliance; (3) Shariah Audit — an assurance function, typically integrated with or reporting alongside the IBI's internal audit, that independently evaluates the adequacy of (1) and (2).
- **AAOIFI standards**: the Accounting and Auditing Organization for Islamic Financial Institutions issues Shariah, accounting, auditing, and governance standards used across the global Islamic finance industry. SBP has directed IBIs in Pakistan to adopt specific AAOIFI Shariah standards on a phased basis, layered on top of (not as a full replacement for) SBP's own SGF and Shariah Board rulings. Always tie an AAOIFI-adoption claim to the specific SBP circular that mandates it rather than assuming blanket applicability.
- **Shariah non-compliance risk**: the risk that a transaction, product, or income stream is later found not to conform to Shariah; non-compliant income typically must be purified (disposed of, often to charity) rather than recognized as bank income. This is a distinct risk category from credit, market, liquidity, or operational risk in Islamic banking risk-management literature.
- **Windows vs. full-fledged**: an Islamic banking window/branch of a conventional bank must maintain segregation of funds and dedicated Shariah oversight even though it operates within a conventional banking group; governance expectations can differ in structural detail from a fully dedicated Islamic bank.

## Extraction Notes

- Always cite the specific SGF circular/year when describing a structural requirement (e.g., Shariah Board minimum size); SBP has revised the SGF multiple times and older figures may no longer be current.
- Do not answer fiqh-level questions ("is this specific Murabaha structure Shariah-compliant") as if they were governance-structure questions; flag that a live Shariah Board or qualified scholar's ruling is required for product-specific permissibility, and that this skill and its author are not a substitute for that.
- Cross-reference `pakistan-sukuk-structuring-issuance` for Sukuk-specific structuring questions, and `sbp-financial-soundness-indicators` for the underlying bank's prudential ratios — this skill covers governance apparatus only.
