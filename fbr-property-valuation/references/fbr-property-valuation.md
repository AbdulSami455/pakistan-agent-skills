# FBR Property Valuation Reference

## Source and Legal Basis

- **Federal Board of Revenue (FBR)** notifies "fair market value" tables for immovable property under **Section 68 of the Income Tax Ordinance, 2001**, via **SROs (Statutory Regulatory Orders)** published in the official gazette.
- FBR's official website is `fbr.gov.pk` (verify) — treat any specific numeric deep-link path (e.g., an SRO number embedded directly in a URL such as `/SROs/1814` or `/Property-Valuation/1805`) as **unverified and needing re-confirmation at retrieval time**. FBR's website structure and internal path/ID numbering have changed over time, and a numeric path that was valid once may 404 or point to unrelated content later. The reliable approach is to search FBR's site for the "Valuation of Immovable Properties" section (sometimes under "Income Tax" or "Broadening of Tax Base" administrative pages) and locate the current SRO list, rather than hard-coding a specific path.
- These FBR-notified values are used as a **floor/reference value** for computing capital gains tax (CGT) on the seller's side and withholding tax (advance tax) on both buyer and seller sides under the Income Tax Ordinance. They are distinct from:
  - **Provincial DC (Deputy Commissioner) rates** — set by provincial revenue/excise & taxation departments, used for provincial stamp duty and registration fee purposes, and historically set lower than actual market value.
  - **Actual market/transaction value** — the price actually paid, which may be higher than both the FBR notified value and the DC rate.
- Since 2016-era reforms, FBR has progressively pushed notified values closer to actual market rates (narrowing the historical gap between DC rates, FBR values, and real market prices), but a gap often still persists depending on the city and area.

## City Coverage — What's Actually Notified

FBR's valuation-table notification program has **not covered the entire country uniformly**. Coverage is concentrated in Pakistan's largest and highest-transaction-volume real estate markets:

- **Tier 1 (most consistently and granularly notified, historically first to get detailed tables):**
  - **Islamabad** — sector-wise and phase-wise tables (e.g., for DHA Islamabad, Bahria Town, F/G/E sectors).
  - **Karachi** — area/scheme-wise tables (e.g., DHA Karachi phases, Clifton, Gulshan-e-Iqbal, Bahria Town Karachi, and other zones).
  - **Lahore** — area/scheme-wise tables (e.g., DHA Lahore phases, Bahria Town Lahore, Gulberg, Model Town, and other zones).

- **Tier 2 (notified, but historically with less granularity or later rollout):**
  - Rawalpindi, Faisalabad, Multan, Peshawar, Quetta, Gujranwala, Sialkot, Hyderabad, Sukkur, Abbottabad, and other divisional/major district headquarters (verify current list — FBR periodically expands the list of notified cities, and the exact set of Tier 2 cities with active tables should be confirmed against the current SRO index rather than assumed static).

- **Not typically covered:** many smaller towns, tehsil-level urban areas, and rural areas generally do not have an FBR-notified valuation table. In such areas, FBR guidance (or the applicable provision of the Ordinance/Rules) typically directs use of the DC rate or another prescribed method as the reference value instead.

Do not assume a valuation table exists for a given city or area without checking; absence of a notified table is common outside the largest cities.

## Revision Cadence

- There is **no fixed statutory schedule** guaranteeing annual revision of every city's table. In practice:
  - FBR has, in recent years, moved toward more frequent revisions (a rough annual-ish cadence in aggregate across all notified cities) as part of an ongoing effort to bring notified values closer to market value and broaden the tax base.
  - However, individual cities' tables have gone multiple years without a fresh SRO, and revisions are **not synchronized** — Islamabad's table might be revised in a given year while Lahore's or a Tier 2 city's table remains unchanged.
  - Revisions are typically triggered by a new SRO specific to one or more cities, not a single nationwide consolidated annual table.
- Always check the **specific SRO's notified/effective date** for the exact city and area in question rather than assuming a rate is current just because it was correct in a recent prior year.

## Valuation Table Structure

- Tables are typically organized by **city → area/scheme/sector/phase → category** (residential plot, commercial plot, constructed property/covered area), with a **rate per unit area** (per square yard, per square foot, or per marla, depending on the city's convention).
- Some tables separate **land value** from a **construction rate** (per square foot of covered area) for built-up property, meaning total notified value = land component + construction component.
- Open plots and constructed properties in the same area often carry different notified rates.

## Extraction Notes

- Always cite the specific SRO number and its notified/effective date when stating a rate, and flag if the exact SRO number cannot be confirmed (verify) rather than presenting an assumed number as fact.
- Confirm whether the city/area in question is actually covered by a notified table before answering — silence in the source material likely means no FBR table exists there, not that the rate is zero or unavailable to check.
- Do not apply one city's or one area's rate to another without explicit confirmation.
- Preserve the unit of measurement exactly as notified; do not silently convert between per-marla, per-square-yard, and per-square-foot without showing the conversion.
- Treat any specific SRO number or URL path referenced in older material as needing re-verification against FBR's current published index before being cited as current.
