# Pakistan Constitution & Governance Reference

## Source

- The Constitution of the Islamic Republic of Pakistan, 1973 (as amended)
- National Assembly of Pakistan official text (as amended, downloadable PDF): `https://na.gov.pk/uploads/documents/1333523681_951.pdf`
- National Assembly downloads section: `https://www.na.gov.pk/en/downloads.php`

## Constitution Structure (General)

- Preamble / Objectives Resolution (as a substantive part per the 8th Amendment).
- Part I: Introductory provisions.
- Part II: Fundamental Rights (Chapter 1) and Principles of Policy (Chapter 2).
- Part III: The Federation of Pakistan (President, federal government, Majlis-e-Shoora/Parliament) and the Provinces.
- Part IV: Relations between Federation and Provinces, including legislative lists/distribution of powers.
- Part V: onward — financial provisions, the judicature (Supreme Court, High Courts, Federal Shariat Court), elections, and amendment procedure (Article 239).

## Key Amendment Milestones

- 18th Amendment (2010): major devolution of powers to provinces, abolition of the Concurrent Legislative List, renaming/restructuring of several institutions.
- Numerous other amendments (1st through 26th+ as of recent years) have modified specific provisions; always check whether a specific Article has been amended before citing its text as current.

## Branches of Government

- Executive: President (head of state, largely ceremonial under the parliamentary system), Prime Minister (head of government), federal cabinet.
- Legislature: bicameral Parliament — National Assembly (directly elected, lower house) and Senate (indirectly elected by provincial assemblies, upper house, equal provincial representation).
- Judiciary: Supreme Court, High Courts (one per province plus Islamabad High Court), Federal Shariat Court, and subordinate/district judiciary.

## Extraction Notes

- Always check whether a cited Article has been affected by a subsequent amendment before presenting it as current text.
- Keep fundamental rights (Chapter 1, judicially enforceable) distinct from principles of policy (Chapter 2, directive).
- Describe federal-provincial power distribution using the post-18th-Amendment framework unless the question specifically asks about an earlier period.
