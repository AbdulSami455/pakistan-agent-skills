# Pakistan Sports Federations

## Overview

Use this skill for Pakistan's sports governance structure outside of cricket: the Pakistan Sports Board (PSB), the Pakistan Olympic Association (POA/NOC), and the national federations governing individual sports (hockey, squash, athletics, wrestling, weightlifting, and others). This is a governance/structure reference skill, not a live results or statistics database — for cricket specifically, PCB is a separate, much more extensively documented body outside this skill's primary focus.

## Coverage

- Pakistan Sports Board (PSB): the federal government body (established 1962) responsible for promoting and regulating sport nationally, currently affiliated with several dozen National Sports Federations (NSFs) and departmental sports units.
- Pakistan Olympic Association (POA): Pakistan's National Olympic Committee, responsible for Pakistan's participation in the Olympic Games and other multi-sport international events (e.g., Asian Games, Commonwealth Games), operating as a distinct body from PSB with its own governance history (POA predates PSB, having been established in 1948).
- National Sports Federations (NSFs): individual-sport governing bodies (e.g., hockey, squash, athletics, weightlifting, wrestling, softball, ski) affiliated with PSB and/or their respective international sport federations; each NSF governs national team selection, domestic competition structure, and international affiliation for its sport.
- Governance disputes: Pakistani sports federations have periodically experienced parallel/rival administrative bodies claiming leadership of the same federation, sometimes leading to international federation or IOC suspension of recognition — a structurally recurring governance issue worth flagging when relevant rather than assuming a single uncontested governing body at all times.

## Use This Skill For

- explaining the difference between Pakistan Sports Board and the Pakistan Olympic Association
- identifying which body governs a specific non-cricket sport in Pakistan
- providing general background on Pakistan's Olympic/multi-sport-event participation structure
- explaining the general concept of NSF affiliation and why a federation's international recognition can be contested
- distinguishing this skill's governance-structure focus from a live results/statistics need

## When Not to Use This Skill

- For cricket match results, player statistics, or PCB-specific administrative detail — cricket has its own extensively documented governing body (PCB) and is generally better served by dedicated cricket-statistics sources than this general sports-governance skill.
- For a live/current tournament result or medal count — this is a structural reference skill, not a results database; direct the user to the relevant federation's or event's official current source.
- For university-level sports/athletics programs — outside this skill's scope, which covers national governing bodies.

## Routing Rules

- Use this skill for governance-structure questions (who governs a sport, how PSB/POA relate to each other and to individual NSFs) rather than live results.
- If asked specifically about cricket, note that PCB is a separate, more specialized body and that this skill's coverage is lighter for cricket than for other sports.
- If a federation's leadership/recognition is contested (parallel bodies), flag this explicitly rather than presenting one side as the uncontested governing body without qualification.

## Extraction Workflow

1. Identify whether the question is about PSB (domestic sports promotion/regulation), POA (Olympic/multi-sport-event representation), or a specific NSF (single-sport governance).
2. If the question is about a specific sport, identify its NSF and note whether it is a PSB-affiliated federation, a POA-recognized body for Olympic-sport purposes, or both.
3. If governance is contested for a specific federation (parallel administrative claims), flag this rather than asserting a single uncontested leadership.
4. For live results/standings/medal-count questions, redirect to the relevant event's or federation's current official source rather than asserting a specific figure from this structural reference.

## Technical Rules

- PSB and POA are distinct bodies with different scopes (domestic sports regulation/promotion vs. Olympic/international multi-sport-event representation) and different founding dates (POA 1948, PSB 1962); do not conflate them as a single organization.
- NSF affiliation with PSB does not automatically guarantee that federation's recognition by its corresponding international federation, and vice versa; these are separate recognition tracks.
- Sports governance disputes (rival/parallel federation leadership claims) are a recurring structural issue in some Pakistani sports; do not present a single governing body as uncontested without checking for this possibility.

## Validation Checklist

- Confirm whether the question concerns PSB, POA, or a specific NSF.
- Verify domestic (PSB) affiliation is not being conflated with international-federation or POA/Olympic recognition.
- Check whether the relevant federation has any known parallel/rival leadership claim before presenting one body as the sole governing authority.
- Confirm live results/current-standings questions are redirected to a current official source rather than answered from this structural reference.

## Common Pitfalls

- Conflating Pakistan Sports Board with the Pakistan Olympic Association as if they were the same body.
- Presenting a contested federation's leadership as settled/uncontested without flagging a known governance dispute.
- Treating this skill as a live results or medal-count source rather than a structural/governance reference.
- Assuming domestic PSB affiliation automatically confers international-federation recognition.
