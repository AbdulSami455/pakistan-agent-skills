# Pakistan Sports Federations Reference

## Source

- Pakistan Sports Board (PSB): `https://sports.gov.pk`
- Pakistan Olympic Association (POA/NOC): `https://nocpakistan.org`
- Individual National Sports Federations (NSFs) typically maintain their own websites/social pages; verify current federation leadership and affiliation status directly with the federation or its international counterpart given periodic governance disputes.

## Governance Structure

- Pakistan Sports Board: federal body established 1962, responsible for domestic sport promotion/regulation, affiliated with dozens of National Sports Federations and departmental sports units.
- Pakistan Olympic Association: Pakistan's National Olympic Committee, established 1948 (predates PSB), responsible for Olympic Games and other multi-sport international event representation (e.g., Asian Games, Commonwealth Games).
- National Sports Federations: single-sport governing bodies (hockey, squash, athletics, weightlifting, wrestling, softball, ski, and others), each responsible for national team selection, domestic competition, and affiliation with the relevant international federation.

## Key Structural Notes

- PSB and POA are separate bodies with different scopes and founding histories; domestic PSB affiliation and Olympic/POA recognition are distinct tracks.
- Some NSFs have experienced parallel/rival administrative bodies claiming leadership, occasionally resulting in international federation or IOC suspension of recognition for the sport in Pakistan — treat any specific federation's leadership as needing current verification rather than assumed stable.
- Cricket (PCB) is administratively and informationally distinct from this general sports-governance structure and is generally better covered by dedicated cricket sources.

## Extraction Notes

- Always identify whether a question concerns PSB, POA, or a specific NSF before answering.
- Flag known or possible governance disputes for a specific federation rather than presenting one leadership claim as uncontested.
- Treat this skill as a structural/governance reference, not a live results or medal-count source.
