# Monetary Policy Statement Reference

## Source

- State Bank of Pakistan, Monetary Policy / MPC decisions section: `https://www.sbp.org.pk`
- Each release normally has a short press release plus a fuller Monetary Policy Statement document for the same meeting.

## Typical Statement Structure

- Opening paragraph: the policy rate decision and effective date.
- Inflation assessment: recent CPI outturns and the MPC's near-term inflation outlook.
- Real sector assessment: growth, large-scale manufacturing, and demand-side commentary.
- External sector assessment: current account, reserves, and exchange-rate context.
- Fiscal commentary: brief reference to fiscal developments where relevant to the inflation/growth outlook.
- Forward guidance: closing language on the expected policy stance, often conditional ("going forward," "the Committee noted that").

## Indicator Guide

- policy rate level and basis-point change
- MPC's inflation outlook range or qualitative characterization
- real GDP growth assessment cited by the MPC
- external account and reserves commentary used to justify the decision
- forward guidance language

## Extraction Notes

- Meeting cadence is irregular (roughly six meetings a year); do not assume fixed quarterly spacing.
- The press release and the full statement may both exist; prefer the full statement for the detailed rationale and the press release for the headline decision if only a quick figure is needed.
- When SBP revises its inflation forecast range, quote the range as stated rather than collapsing it to a single number.
- Track whether the MPC characterizes risks to the outlook as "balanced," "tilted to the upside," or "tilted to the downside" since this is a recurring, meaningful phrase.
