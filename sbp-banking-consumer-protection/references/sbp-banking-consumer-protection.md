# SBP Banking Consumer Protection Reference

## Source

- State Bank of Pakistan (SBP), Consumer Protection function: `https://www.sbp.org.pk/our-operations/consumer-protection`
- Banking Mohtasib Pakistan (BMP), independent statutory banking ombudsman: `https://www.bankingmohtasib.gov.pk`

## Process Structure

- First tier: the customer's own bank Complaint Management Unit (CMU) — required first point of contact for any banking complaint.
- Second tier: Banking Mohtasib Pakistan (BMP) — available if the CMU fails to respond within the specified period (commonly cited as around 45 days) or the customer is unsatisfied with the CMU's resolution.
- BMP operates under the Federal Ombudsman Institutional Reforms Act (FOIRA), 2013, with a largely conciliatory dispute-resolution approach.

## Scope

- Violations of banking law/regulation.
- Excessive delays or inefficiency in service.
- Poor service quality.
- Discriminatory treatment.
- Other banking-service conduct issues within BMP's statutory mandate.

## Key Process Notes

- BMP's service is free of charge and does not require legal representation.
- The general expectation is CMU-first, then BMP if unresolved — this escalation order should be described accurately rather than skipped.

## Extraction Notes

- Always describe the CMU-then-BMP escalation sequence rather than presenting BMP as a first-resort channel.
- Keep this skill's individual-consumer-dispute focus distinct from SBP's monetary-policy (`sbp-monetary-policy-statement`), bank-soundness (`sbp-financial-soundness-indicators`), and financial-stability (`sbp-financial-stability-review`) functions, which are separate SBP roles covered by other skills.
- Note that non-bank financial complaints (e.g., insurance) may fall under a different ombudsman/regulator outside BMP's specifically banking-sector scope.
