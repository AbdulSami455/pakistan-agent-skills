# PSDP Federal Development Programme Reference

## Source

- Ministry of Planning, Development & Special Initiatives (MoPD&SI), Government of Pakistan
- Planning Commission website: `https://www.pc.gov.pk`
- PSDP documents typically published under "Public Sector Development Programme" or "Development Budget" sections
- Periodic release/expenditure reports may be published on the Ministry website or through the Finance Division

## Typical Document Structure

- Total PSDP size and comparison with previous year
- Ministry/division-wise allocation summary
- Sector-wise breakdown (infrastructure, social sectors, production sectors)
- Project-wise detail: project name, executing agency, estimated cost, allocation, cumulative expenditure
- Foreign-aided vs. locally funded split
- Releases and expenditure reports (issued during the fiscal year)

## Key Distinctions

- **Allocation**: budgeted amount for the fiscal year (forward-looking at budget time)
- **Release**: funds actually released to executing agencies during the year
- **Expenditure**: amount actually spent against released funds
- **Estimated cost**: total project cost across all years, not the same as annual allocation

## Extraction Notes

- Always anchor a figure to its fiscal year and to whether it is allocation, release, or expenditure.
- For project lookups, use the project-wise table; ministry aggregates alone may not identify a specific project.
- Provincial ADPs are separate documents maintained by provincial planning departments.
