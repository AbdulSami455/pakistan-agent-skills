# Karachi Port Cargo Reports Reference

## Source

- Karachi Port Trust (KPT)
- KPT official website: `https://www.kpt.gov.pk`
- Daily shipping notices, cargo statistics, and trade data published on the KPT website under "Shipping" or "Trade & Traffic" sections

## Typical Report Fields

- Vessel name, type, arrival/berthing date, berth/terminal assignment
- Cargo type and quantity handled per vessel
- Terminal-wise cumulative throughput (daily/monthly/fiscal-year-to-date)
- Commodity categories: containers (TEU), bulk grain, coal, POL products, general cargo

## Named Terminals at Karachi Port

- **KICT — Karachi International Container Terminal**: a major container terminal operator at Karachi Port.
- **PICT — Pakistan International Container Terminal**: another major container terminal at Karachi Port.
- **SAPT — South Asia Pakistan Terminal**: a deep-water container terminal at Karachi Port able to handle larger vessels than the port's older berths.
- Karachi Port also has general-cargo, dry-bulk, and liquid-cargo berths operated directly by Karachi Port Trust, in addition to the dedicated container terminal operators above.
- Do NOT list Qasim International Container Terminal (QICT), Pakistan International Bulk Terminal (PIBT), or the Engro LNG terminals here — these belong to Port Qasim, a physically and administratively separate port (see `port-qasim-cargo-reports`). PIBT in particular is sometimes miscited as a Karachi Port facility; it is at Port Qasim.

## Scale Note

- Karachi Port is Pakistan's oldest and largest seaport and has historically handled the majority share of the country's seaborne trade by volume, ahead of Port Qasim and Gwadar. Keep this comparison qualitative — do not cite a specific TEU, tonnage, or market-share percentage figure unless it comes directly from the report being read, since exact shares shift year to year and by cargo type.

## Distinct Ports Note

- Karachi Port (Karachi Port Trust), Port Qasim (Port Qasim Authority), and Gwadar Port (Gwadar Port Authority) are separately operated and separately reported; do not merge their statistics without explicit aggregation from an authoritative source.

## Extraction Notes

- Always anchor a figure to its exact date/period and to Karachi Port specifically.
- Container volumes (TEU) and bulk/liquid cargo volumes (metric tons) are different units and should not be combined into a single "cargo" figure without conversion methodology.
