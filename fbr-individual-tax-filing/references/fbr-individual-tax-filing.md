# FBR Individual Tax Filing Reference

## Source

- Federal Board of Revenue (FBR), Government of Pakistan
- FBR official website: `https://www.fbr.gov.pk`
- IRIS portal (return filing, e-payments, registration): `https://iris.fbr.gov.pk`
- File Income Tax Return guidance page: `https://fbr.gov.pk/categ/file-income-tax-return/51147/80860/71158`

## Process Structure

- NTN registration: individuals register using their CNIC as the basis for their National Tax Number within IRIS.
- Annual income tax return: filed through IRIS, with forms varying by taxpayer category (salaried individual, business individual/AOP, or combined income sources).
- Wealth statement: an annual declaration of assets and liabilities filed alongside the income tax return for individuals meeting the applicable filing threshold.
- Active Taxpayer List (ATL): published list of taxpayers who have filed their return for the relevant tax year; ATL inclusion confers "filer" status with generally lower withholding tax rates on various transactions compared to "non-filers."
- e-Payment: tax due can be paid through IRIS-integrated payment channels.

## Key Concepts

- Tax year: generally July-June for most individuals, with rates/slabs set annually via the Finance Act.
- Filer vs. non-filer: filer status depends on current-year ATL inclusion, not merely having registered for an NTN in the past.
- IRIS 2.0: the current version of FBR's online tax portal, succeeding earlier IRIS/eFBR interfaces.

## Extraction Notes

- Always anchor any tax rate, slab, or deadline to a specific tax year, since these are revised annually.
- Do not equate NTN registration with filer status; filer status requires current ATL inclusion.
- Keep the wealth statement and income tax return conceptually distinct even though they are typically filed together.
- Recommend consulting FBR's official current notifications or a tax professional for a specific individual's complex filing situation rather than treating this reference as exhaustive tax advice.
