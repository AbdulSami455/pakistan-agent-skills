# NDMA SITREPs Reference

## Source

- National Disaster Management Authority (NDMA), Government of Pakistan
- NDMA official website: `https://www.ndma.gov.pk`
- SITREPs section: `https://www.ndma.gov.pk/sitreps`
- Publications archive: `https://www.ndma.gov.pk/publication_by_category/2`

## Document Structure

- Header: SITREP number, issuing date, and event/season label (e.g., "Monsoon 2025").
- Rainfall and meteorological summary for the reporting period, often citing Pakistan Meteorological Department (PMD) data.
- Province/district-wise impact table: fatalities, injuries, houses damaged/destroyed.
- Livestock losses and infrastructure damage (roads, bridges) where applicable.
- Relief and response section: relief camps established, persons sheltered, rescue operations, and resources deployed.
- Cumulative season-to-date totals, typically presented alongside the current reporting period's incremental figures.

## Key Indicator Types

- fatalities and injuries (incremental and cumulative)
- houses damaged/destroyed
- livestock losses
- infrastructure damage (roads, bridges, other)
- relief camps and persons sheltered
- rescue operations conducted

## Extraction Notes

- SITREPs are issued sequentially during an active event and discontinued once the authority determines the emergency phase has ended (e.g., after monsoon withdrawal); the last-issued SITREP typically carries the final cumulative tally for that season.
- Figures are provisional at time of issue and can be revised in later SITREPs as verification continues — prefer the most recent SITREP when figures conflict.
- NDMA also issues situation reports for non-flood disasters (earthquakes, GLOF events, landslides) using a similar numbering and structure; confirm which event a SITREP belongs to before using it.
- Cross-reference with `irsa-reservoir-data` only for hydrological context (reservoir levels/flows); do not merge IRSA's operational data into NDMA's casualty/damage figures.
