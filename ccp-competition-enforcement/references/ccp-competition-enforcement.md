# CCP Competition Enforcement Reference

## Source

- Competition Commission of Pakistan (CCP) official website: `https://www.cc.gov.pk` (verify current domain — CCP has used `cc.gov.pk` historically; confirm before citing as a live link).
- CCP publishes: Orders/decisions (by Bench), an Annual Report, Merger Control Regulations, and press releases/news items summarizing enforcement actions.
- Governing statute: The Competition Act, 2010 (Act No. XIX of 2010), preceded by the Competition Ordinance, 2007 (which was re-promulgated several times before the 2010 Act was passed by Parliament).

## Institutional Structure

- CCP is headed by a Chairperson and Members, and decides cases through Bench(es) constituted from among its Members — similar in structure to other quasi-judicial regulatory commissions in Pakistan (e.g., NEPRA, OGRA, PTA), but with a subject-matter mandate limited to competition/antitrust, not sector-specific licensing or tariff-setting.
- CCP has an Office of Fair Trade / investigative wings that conduct inquiries before a formal show-cause notice and Bench hearing.

## Core Statutory Provisions (Competition Act, 2010)

| Provision | Subject | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Section 3 | Abuse of dominant position | Prohibits a dominant undertaking from abusing its position — e.g., predatory pricing, unfair trading conditions, limiting production/markets, tying/bundling. |
| Section 4 | Prohibited agreements | Prohibits agreements between undertakings (including cartels) that have the object or effect of restricting competition — price-fixing, market allocation, bid-rigging, output restriction. |

## Merger Control

- CCP operates a **pre-merger notification** regime: parties to a transaction meeting notified asset-value or turnover thresholds must notify CCP and obtain clearance before completing the merger/acquisition.
- Thresholds are set out in CCP's **Merger Control Regulations** (originally issued around 2007, subsequently revised) and are denominated in Rupees; these thresholds are revised periodically — **do not cite a specific Rupee threshold figure as current without checking CCP's latest Merger Control (Amendment) Regulations**, as this repo intentionally avoids presenting a potentially stale number as a hard fact.
- CCP's possible outcomes on a merger review: unconditional approval (Phase I), approval subject to conditions/remedies (structural or behavioral), or a Phase II detailed review that can result in blocking the transaction.
- Historic CCP merger reviews have covered sectors including cement, banking, telecom, and consumer goods — (verify specific deal names/years against CCP's published merger order list before citing a particular transaction).

## CCP Annual Report

- CCP publishes an Annual Report summarizing cases initiated, inquiries concluded, orders passed, penalties imposed, and merger reviews completed for the reporting year.
- Use the Annual Report as the primary aggregate source for "how many cases did CCP handle in year X" type questions, rather than summing individually recalled cases, which risks omission or double-counting.

## Appeal Path

- A party aggrieved by a CCP Order may appeal to the **Competition Appellate Tribunal (CAT)**, a distinct appellate body established under the Competition Act.
- Further appeal from the CAT lies to the ordinary superior judiciary (High Court and/or Supreme Court), consistent with Pakistan's general appellate hierarchy.
- (Verify the Competition Appellate Tribunal's current operational/staffing status before asserting that an appeal is actively being heard — the Tribunal's functioning has experienced periods of vacancy/disruption historically; confirm current status against CCP or CAT's own published material.)
- A CCP order that is under appeal should not be described as final or conclusively enforceable; note the appeal status explicitly.

## Disambiguation: CCP vs. SECP

| | CCP (Competition Commission of Pakistan) | SECP (Securities and Exchange Commission of Pakistan) |
|---|---|---|
| Governing law | Competition Act, 2010 | SECP Act, 1997; Companies Act, 2017 |
| Mandate | Market competition / antitrust across **all sectors** | Securities markets, corporate registration/regulation, insurance, non-bank finance |
| Typical output | Orders on mergers, cartels, abuse of dominance, deceptive marketing | Company registration records, corporate filings, securities-market regulation, insurance sector oversight |
| Scope limiter | Not limited to listed/registered companies — applies to any "undertaking" | Companies Act compliance and securities-market participants |
| Related skill in this repo | this skill | `secp-company-registry`, `secp-insurance-industry-statistics` |

Always disambiguate explicitly when a question uses "commission" ambiguously — CCP and SECP are entirely separate statutory bodies with separate laws, separate leadership, and separate published data.

## Extraction Notes

- Always cite the specific Competition Act provision (Section 3, 4, or 10) rather than a generic "antitrust violation" label.
- Always state whether an order is final, stayed, or under appeal.
- Do not conflate a CCP penalty order with a criminal conviction — CCP proceedings are regulatory/administrative, not criminal prosecutions.
- Cross-reference `secp-company-registry` only for company existence/registration questions; do not use SECP sources to answer a CCP competition question or vice versa.
