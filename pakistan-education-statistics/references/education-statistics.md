# Pakistan Education Statistics Reference

## Source

- AEPAM (Academy of Educational Planning and Management) "Pakistan Education Statistics" annual report
- Underlying data from NEMIS (National Education Management Information System) Annual School Census (ASC)
- Published by the Ministry of Federal Education and Professional Training
- AEPAM merged into the Pakistan Institute of Education (PIE) in 2021; PIE website: `https://pie.gov.pk`
- NEMIS/EMIS portal: `https://www.emis.gob.pk`
- Ministry of Federal Education: `https://mofept.gov.pk`

## Document Structure

- National summary chapter: total institutions, total enrollment, total teachers, and headline year-over-year change.
- Level-wise chapters/sections: separate detailed tables for primary, middle, secondary, and higher secondary/intermediate levels.
- Province-wise breakdown within each level: Punjab, Sindh, Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, Balochistan, Islamabad Capital Territory (ICT), Azad Jammu & Kashmir (AJK), and Gilgit-Baltistan; some editions also separately report former FATA/merged districts.
- Cross-cutting tables: gender splits (boys/girls/co-education institutions and enrollment), public/private sector splits, rural/urban splits, and facility-availability indicators (electricity, drinking water, boundary wall, toilets) where collected.
- Some editions include a student-teacher ratio table and a teacher-qualification table.

## Key Indicator Types

- number of institutions by level, province, gender, and sector
- enrollment by level, province, gender, and sector
- number of teachers by level, province, gender, and sector
- student-teacher ratio
- school facility availability indicators

## Extraction Notes

- ASC editions are dated to a specific academic year (e.g. "2023-24"); always carry that year label with any cited figure.
- Institutions, enrollment, and teachers are reported in separate tables — do not infer one from another without an explicit derived-ratio table.
- ICT, AJK, and Gilgit-Baltistan reporting scope can vary by edition; confirm whether a "national" total includes or excludes them.
- Public/private and rural/urban splits are not always available at every level; check the specific table before assuming the breakdown exists.
