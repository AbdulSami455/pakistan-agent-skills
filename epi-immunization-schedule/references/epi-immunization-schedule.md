# EPI Immunization Schedule Reference

## Source

- Federal Directorate of Immunization (FDI), Pakistan, implementing the Expanded Programme on Immunization (EPI)
- FDI official website: `https://www.epi.gov.pk`
- Immunization schedule page: `https://www.epi.gov.pk/immunization-schedule/`
- WHO EMRO's Pakistan EPI programme page provides additional international-partner context: `https://www.emro.who.int/pak/programmes/expanded-programme-immunization.html`
- Provincial EPI programs (e.g., Sindh EPI, Punjab EPI) implement the national schedule with their own delivery-specific portals.

## Programme Background

- Launched in Pakistan in 1978 in partnership with WHO.
- Targets 12 vaccine-preventable diseases (VPDs) in children aged 0-15 months, plus a maternal tetanus/Td component for pregnant women.
- Schedule has expanded incrementally: hepatitis B (2002), Hib (2009), PCV (2012), inactivated polio vaccine (2015), rotavirus (2017), typhoid conjugate vaccine (phased provincially, Sindh 2019, Punjab/Islamabad 2021), measles-rubella (MR) vaccine replacing standalone measles (2021).

## Document Structure

- Age-based schedule table: antigen(s) given at birth and at subsequent age milestones through approximately 15 months.
- Antenatal tetanus/Td schedule for pregnant women, tied to maternal and neonatal tetanus elimination.
- Vaccine-preventable disease list, mapping each antigen to the disease(s) it targets.

## Extraction Notes

- Always confirm whether a question needs the current schedule or reflects a historical point before a specific antigen was added.
- Distinguish the fixed, age-based routine schedule from supplementary immunization activities (e.g., polio campaign rounds), which run on separate campaign-specific timing.
- Note any provincial phase-in difference for a given antigen rather than assuming simultaneous nationwide introduction.
- Cross-reference `who-pakistan-health-statistics` only for coverage-rate/international-indicator questions, and `nih-disease-surveillance` only for outbreak/case-count questions — this skill covers the prescribed schedule itself.
