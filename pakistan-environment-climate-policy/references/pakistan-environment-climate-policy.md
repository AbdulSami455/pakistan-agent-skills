# Pakistan Environment & Climate Policy Reference

## Source

- Pakistan Environmental Protection Agency (Pak-EPA): `https://environment.gov.pk`
- Ministry of Climate Change and Environmental Coordination: `https://www.mocc.gov.pk`
- Provincial EPAs (e.g., Gilgit-Baltistan's Environmental Protection Agency, and similar bodies in Punjab, Sindh, KP, Balochistan) operate separately under provincial legislation; confirm the correct provincial site for a jurisdiction-specific question.

## Institutional Structure

- Pak-EPA: federal executive agency, operational since 1997, under the Ministry of Climate Change's policy oversight; responsible for EIA/IEE review and approval, pollution-control standards, and enforcement.
- Provincial EPAs: post-18th Amendment, environmental protection is largely a devolved/provincial subject; each province generally runs its own EPA with its own EIA/IEE process for projects within its jurisdiction.
- Ministry of Climate Change and Environmental Coordination: federal policy ministry responsible for national climate policy, international climate negotiations/commitments (e.g., Pakistan's NDCs under the Paris Agreement), and cross-cutting climate-adaptation/mitigation frameworks.

## EIA/IEE Process

- Initial Environmental Examination (IEE): review track for projects with lower expected environmental impact.
- Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA): more extensive review track for projects with higher expected environmental impact.
- Project proponents submit the applicable assessment for regulatory review; approval may include conditions or required project modifications.
