# SUPARCO Reference

## Source

- Space and Upper Atmosphere Research Commission (SUPARCO), Pakistan's national space agency
- SUPARCO official website: `https://suparco.gov.pk` (see the site's "About Us" navigation for organizational background; specific sub-page URLs can change between site updates)

## Organizational Background

- Established as a committee in 1961; granted Commission status in 1981.
- Headquartered in Islamabad, with technical facilities/ground stations in Karachi, Lahore, Multan, Quetta, Peshawar, and Gilgit.
- Mandate: research and development in space science, technology, and applications for peaceful purposes and national socio-economic development.

## Programme Areas

- Satellite communications: geostationary communications satellites (e.g., the PAKSAT series) supporting broadcast relay and connectivity applications.
- Remote sensing / earth observation: satellites (e.g., the PRSS series) supporting agriculture monitoring, disaster management, land-use mapping, and resource assessment.
- Space science and upper-atmosphere research.
- International cooperation: partnerships with other national space agencies on specific satellite development, launch, and (more recently) human-spaceflight-related cooperation.

## Extraction Notes

- Distinguish communications-satellite programs from remote-sensing-satellite programs when describing a specific mission's purpose.
- Treat specific mission operational/technical details as needing verification against SUPARCO's current official announcements.
- Cross-reference `pta-telecom-indicators` only for telecom-sector regulation questions and `pmd-weather-climate-data` only for weather-forecasting questions — SUPARCO's mandate is space research/technology, distinct from both.
