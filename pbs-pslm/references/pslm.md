# PSLM Reference

## Source

- Pakistan Bureau of Statistics (PBS), "Pakistan Social and Living Standards Measurement" (PSLM) survey
- Published as a key findings volume plus detailed statistical tables
- PBS official website: `https://www.pbs.gov.pk`
- PSLM reports: `https://www.pbs.gov.pk/content/pslm-district-level-surveys`

## Document Structure

- Household demographic and education module.
- Health module (morbidity, immunization, health-seeking behavior).
- Water supply and sanitation module.
- Housing characteristics module (unit type, room count, tenure, amenities).
- Household consumption/expenditure module, used to derive official poverty headcount estimates.
- Access-to-services indicators spanning education, health facilities, water, and electricity.
- Survey rounds alternate between a national/provincial round (larger sample) and a district-level round (used for district estimates); not every round produces district-level figures.

## Key Indicator Types

- poverty headcount rate (national, provincial, district where available)
- household consumption/expenditure statistics (mean, median, distributional)
- access-to-services indicators (water, sanitation, electricity, education, health)
- housing characteristics
- health and education module indicators (immunization, school attendance, literacy)

## Extraction Notes

- Always confirm whether the survey round generated district-level estimates before citing a district figure.
- Poverty line methodology can be revised between rounds; flag any methodology change before comparing poverty rates across years.
- Treat PSLM as a distinct survey instrument from the PBS Labour Force Survey, even where modules overlap in demographic coverage.
- Distinguish household-level percentages from individual-level percentages when citing access-to-services indicators.
