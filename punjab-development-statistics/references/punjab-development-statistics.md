# Punjab Development Statistics Reference

## Source

- Punjab Bureau of Statistics, Government of Punjab
- Published annually as "Punjab Development Statistics" (also issued as "Punjab in Figures")
- Official website: `https://bos.punjab.gov.pk` (Bureau of Statistics, Government of Punjab)
- Publications section: `https://bos.punjab.gov.pk/publicationreports`
- Alongside the full "Punjab Development Statistics" volume, the Bureau also publishes a shorter, headline-indicator companion booklet each year (the skill description and this reference refer to it as "Punjab in Figures" — (verify) the exact current title, as Punjab BOS has used variant naming across editions, e.g. some years' condensed booklets have circulated under slightly different cover titles). Treat any such condensed booklet as a quick-reference summary only; it typically presents current-year headline figures without the multi-year time series or full district/division tables found in the main volume.

## Administrative Geography

- Punjab is organized into **9 divisions**, each grouping multiple districts: **Lahore**, **Gujranwala**, **Faisalabad**, **Rawalpindi**, **Multan**, **Bahawalpur**, **Dera Ghazi Khan**, **Sargodha**, **Sahiwal**.
- Lahore division includes the provincial capital (Lahore city) and is typically the most urbanized; Bahawalpur and Dera Ghazi Khan divisions cover the province's more arid southern belt (Cholistan desert areas fall within Bahawalpur division). Division boundaries occasionally shift as new divisions/districts are notified — confirm the edition's boundary vintage before treating a division list as current.
- District count within Punjab has grown over time via splits (e.g., new districts carved from larger historical ones); the Development Statistics volume's district-level tables should be checked against the specific edition's stated district list rather than assumed to match an older or newer count.

## Extraction Notes

- Always anchor a figure to its administrative level (district/division/province) and reference year.
- "Punjab in Figures" is a quick-reference summary; for full time series and district breakdowns, the main Development Statistics volume is the authoritative source.
