# NCCIA & PECA Reference

## Agency Timeline (Critical Currency Note)

- **2007**: FIA establishes the National Response Centre for Cyber Crime (NR3C) as its cybercrime investigation wing, initially operating under earlier electronic-crime legal instruments (e.g., the Electronic Transactions Ordinance, 2002, and later the Prevention of Electronic Crimes Ordinance, 2007/2009 era) predating the 2016 Act.
- **2016**: Prevention of Electronic Crimes Act (PECA), 2016 enacted, becoming Pakistan's principal cybercrime statute; NR3C (under FIA) continues as the primary investigative body enforcing it.
- **April 2025**: The National Cyber Crime Investigation Agency (NCCIA) is established as a standalone federal agency, superseding/absorbing NR3C's role as Pakistan's primary cybercrime investigation and enforcement body under PECA.
- **2025 PECA amendment**: A contemporaneous/related amendment to PECA expanded the law's scope, particularly around disinformation and online content regulation, and was publicly controversial (raising concerns from journalists, civil society, and digital rights groups about potential overreach into online speech). Treat specific clause numbers, exact penalty figures, and precise definitional language from this amendment as "(verify against the current Gazette-notified amendment text)" — this is a fast-moving and contested area where secondary reporting has varied.

**Operating rule**: Any content — training data, cached knowledge, or a user's own prior assumption — that refers to "NR3C" or "FIA's cybercrime wing" as the *current* body handling PECA enforcement is describing the pre-April-2025 structure. Correct this proactively.

## PECA 2016 — Offense Categories (General Structure)

- **Unauthorized access to information systems or data**: accessing or attempting to access a system/data without authorization.
- **Unauthorized copying or transmission of data**: distinct from mere access, covering exfiltration/transmission of data without authorization.
- **Electronic fraud**: dishonestly/fraudulently causing a wrongful loss or gain via an information system (e.g., online financial fraud, phishing-enabled fraud).
- **Electronic forgery**: interference with data/documents in a way that creates a false electronic document/record.
- **Cyberterrorism**: certain serious offenses under the Act committed with intent to advance specified terrorism-related purposes or against critical infrastructure, carrying enhanced penalties.
- **Cyberstalking / harassment**: using an information system to repeatedly contact, monitor, or harass a person.
- **Identity theft / spoofing**: dishonestly using another person's identity information via an information system.
- **Offenses adjacent to hate speech / defamation**: provisions addressing glorification of an offense/offender, or use of an information system to defame/harm the reputation or privacy of a person; this category has been the subject of the most public debate over scope and potential misuse, especially after the 2025 amendment.
- Note: exact section numbers and penalty ranges (fines, imprisonment terms) should be verified against the current text of PECA 2016 as amended, since this reference intentionally avoids asserting specific section-by-section penalty figures that could be stated with false precision.

## NCCIA — Role and Scope

- NCCIA is the standalone federal agency now responsible for investigating and enforcing PECA offenses, established via legal/administrative action in April 2025.
- Its mandate is offense-specific: it handles matters falling under PECA's defined offense categories, not general federal criminal jurisdiction.
- NCCIA's establishment was framed as replacing/absorbing FIA's NR3C function specifically for this mandate — verify the precise administrative mechanism (e.g., whether via a new standalone statute, an executive/administrative order, or an amendment to PECA itself establishing the agency) against current official sources, as this reference is describing the general shift rather than citing the specific enabling instrument verbatim.

## Jurisdictional Boundaries — Who Handles What

- **NCCIA**: PECA-specific offenses (as above).
- **FIA (general)**: retains jurisdiction over other federal crimes not falling under PECA (e.g., immigration/human trafficking offenses, certain financial crimes not routed through NCCIA) — FIA and NCCIA are distinct in mandate even if historically related through NR3C's origin inside FIA.
- **NAB (National Accountability Bureau)**: handles corruption/accountability matters (misuse of authority, corrupt practices by public officeholders, large-scale financial corruption) — a cyber-enabled corruption case may have both a PECA/NCCIA investigative dimension (the electronic-crime mechanism) and a separate NAB dimension (the underlying corruption/accountability question). See `nab-accountability-data` for the latter.
- **Provincial police**: retains jurisdiction over conventional (non-electronic) crime under respective provincial police laws; not a substitute for NCCIA on PECA-specific matters.
