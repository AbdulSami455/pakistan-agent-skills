## Source

- Pakistan Economic Survey 2025-26 PDF
- Official source provided by user: `https://www.finance.gov.pk/survey/chapter_26/Complete_PES2025_26.pdf`

## Chapter Map

- Fiscal Development: survey page `63`
- Money and Credit: survey page `83`
- Capital Markets & Corporate Sector: survey page `103`
- Public Debt: survey page `155`

## Section Entry Points

- Fiscal Development contains component-wise expenditure tables around survey pages `65-67`.
- Use Table `4.1` around survey page `65` for `% of GDP` spending composition.
- Money and Credit contains `5.2 Monetary Policy Stance in Pakistan` around survey page `84`.
- Use `5.3 Recent Monetary and Credit` around survey pages `85-88` for banking-system assets, borrowing, and private-sector credit.
- Capital Markets & Corporate Sector contains `6.1 Equity Market` around survey page `103`.
- Use `6.1-a Performance and Structure`, `6.1-b Sector-wise Market Capitalization`, and `6.2 Debt Markets` around survey pages `104-109` for PSX and market indicators.
- Public Debt starts at survey page `155`; use it when the question requires debt-specific detail rather than fiscal aggregates alone.

## Key Indicator Types

- fiscal deficit
- primary surplus or primary balance
- current and development expenditure
- markup payments
- policy rate and reserve requirement changes
- market capitalization, index, and corporate market indicators
- debt trends and servicing discussion

## Extraction Notes

- Preserve `% of GDP`, `basis points`, `Rs`, and `US $` exactly.
- Keep table context visible when FY actuals and budget estimates are presented together.
- Use chapter-specific language rather than blending fiscal and debt narratives into one unsourced claim.
- Fiscal tables often place `FY 2026 (B.E)` next to earlier actual years; do not quote budget estimates as realized outcomes.
- Monetary sections can mix current policy actions with cumulative easing or tightening since earlier years; keep both timelines separate.
