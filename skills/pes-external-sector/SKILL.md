# PES External Sector

## Overview

Use this skill for Pakistan Economic Survey 2025-26 questions about the external sector, including exports, imports, remittances, current account performance, reserves, and external vulnerability. This skill is the right entry point when the task is about trade performance or balance-of-payments style interpretation inside the survey.

## Chapter Coverage

- Chapter 8: Trade and Payments: starts at survey page `137`

## Use This Skill For

- extracting current account figures
- summarizing export and import trends
- discussing remittance performance
- analyzing reserve developments or external buffers
- explaining survey treatment of external shocks such as freight, oil, or market disruptions

## Routing Rules

- Use this skill whenever the question is about the balance between external inflows and outflows.
- If the question crosses into public external debt, use this skill for trade and reserves context and the fiscal or debt skill for debt stock detail.
- If the question is primarily about IT export support measures or digital sector policy, read the infrastructure and digital skill after this one.

## Extraction Workflow

1. Identify whether the question is about trade flows, remittances, current account, reserves, or policy measures for external sustainability.
2. Extract the exact sub-period, such as `July-March FY 2026` or `April 2026`.
3. Preserve whether the figure is a surplus or deficit and keep the sign explicit.
4. Keep all currency units exactly as reported.
5. Separate pre-shock and post-shock discussion if the chapter does so.

## Technical Rules

- Do not collapse goods exports, services exports, remittances, and reserves into one undifferentiated “external performance” number.
- Preserve event timing when the chapter links external shifts to the Middle East crisis or freight and oil-price pressures.
- Keep current account values distinct from reserves and official inflows.
- When the chapter discusses measures, separate policy actions from outcomes already realized.

## Validation Checklist

- Check whether the number refers to a sub-period or the full fiscal year narrative.
- Verify whether the chapter is discussing exports, imports, current account, or reserves before quoting a figure.
- Confirm whether the figure is stated in millions or billions of US dollars.
- Avoid attributing external improvement to one factor if the survey names several.

## Common Pitfalls

- Confusing current account surplus with reserve accumulation.
- Mixing export weakness in one category with overall external collapse language the survey does not use.
- Losing the month or period context for deficit or surplus values.
- Treating policy-support measures as already delivered outcomes.
