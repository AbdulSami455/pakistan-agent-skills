# Azad Jammu & Kashmir Development Statistics Reference

## Source

- Bureau of Statistics, Planning & Development Department, Government of Azad Jammu & Kashmir
- Published annually as "AJK Development Statistics" (sometimes also issued in condensed form as "AJK in Figures")
- Official website: `https://ajk.gov.pk` (Planning & Development / Bureau of Statistics section) — (verify) this domain and section path have not been independently confirmed as the current, correct home for the AJK Bureau of Statistics. - Publications section: check the Bureau's "Publications" or "Statistical Reports" page for the latest edition — (verify) exact path.

## Constitutional and Administrative Status

- AJK is **not a province** of Pakistan. It is a self-governing territory with its own President, Prime Minister, Legislative Assembly, and judiciary, administered under the **Azad Jammu and Kashmir Interim Constitution Act, 1974** (as amended). This is distinct from the four provinces (Punjab, Sindh, KP, Balochistan), which operate under Pakistan's 1973 Constitution.
- Because AJK sits outside Pakistan's four-province federal structure, its statistics are compiled separately by its own Bureau of Statistics under the AJK Planning & Development Department rather than by a provincial bureau, and AJK is frequently **excluded, footnoted, or reported separately** in national Pakistan Bureau of Statistics (PBS) compilations and in the Pakistan Economic Survey. Do not assume AJK figures are folded into "Pakistan" national totals without checking the source table's coverage note.
- This separate-compilation structure is the main reason AJK-specific indicators (e.g., census counts, labour force figures) may lag, differ in methodology, or simply be absent from PBS national releases — treat the AJK Bureau of Statistics report as the primary source for AJK figures rather than trying to back them out of national tables.

## Administrative Geography

- AJK is organized into **3 divisions and 10 districts** (the district count has grown over time via splits from older, larger districts; confirm which districts a given edition enumerates before comparing across years):
  - **Muzaffarabad division**: Muzaffarabad (capital), Neelum, Hattian Bala
  - **Mirpur division**: Mirpur, Bhimber, Kotli
  - **Poonch division**: Poonch (headquartered at Rawalakot), Bagh, Sudhnoti, Haveli
- Muzaffarabad is the capital of AJK. Mirpur district has significant UK/overseas diaspora ties, which is often a relevant contextual note for remittance-, finance-, and labour-related indicators specific to that district.
- Older editions of the Development Statistics report may show fewer districts (e.g., 8 or 9) since Haveli and Hattian Bala are more recent splits from Poonch and Muzaffarabad respectively; always confirm the boundary vintage before building a district-level time series.

## Extraction Notes

- Always anchor a figure to its administrative level (district/territory) and reference year.
- Condensed "in Figures" booklets are quick-reference summaries; for full time series and district breakdowns, the main Development Statistics volume is the authoritative source.
- AJK is a distinct administrative territory, not a province; preserve this label when citing figures.
