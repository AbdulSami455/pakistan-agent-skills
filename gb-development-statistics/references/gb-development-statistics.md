- Published annually as "Gilgit-Baltistan Development Statistics" (sometimes also issued in condensed form as "GB in Figures")

## Constitutional and Administrative Status

- GB is **not a province** of Pakistan and has disputed/special constitutional status tied to the broader Jammu & Kashmir dispute. It is administered under the **Gilgit-Baltistan Governance Order** (historically the 2009 Gilgit-Baltistan Empowerment and Self-Governance Order, since revised) rather than under Pakistan's 1973 Constitution as a full province. GB has its own Legislative Assembly and a Chief Minister, but its constitutional integration into Pakistan's federal structure remains distinct and periodically revisited by governance orders/reforms.
- Because of this special status, GB is categorized **separately from the four provinces** in most national compilations — PBS and Pakistan Economic Survey tables often list GB (like AJK) as a distinct category outside the provincial breakdown, or omit it from provincial totals entirely. Do not assume GB figures are included in "national" or "all-provinces" totals without checking the source table's coverage note.
- Treat the GB Planning & Development Department / Bureau of Statistics report as the primary source for GB-specific figures rather than assuming national PBS releases disaggregate down to GB.

## Administrative Geography

- GB is organized into **2 divisions and 10 districts** (district count has grown through splits over time; confirm which districts a given edition enumerates before comparing years):
  - **Gilgit division**: Gilgit, Hunza, Nagar, Ghizer
  - **Baltistan division**: Skardu, Ghanche, Shigar, Kharmang, Roundu (a more recently created district split from Skardu — verify inclusion in the edition being used)
  - **Diamer division** (sometimes grouped separately or under Gilgit division depending on the source): Diamer, Astore
- Gilgit is the capital and largest city of GB. Skardu is the largest city/district in Baltistan and a major tourism gateway.
- GB's mountainous, high-altitude terrain and dispersed small settlements (similar in kind to Balochistan's sampling issue but driven by terrain rather than sheer area) make district-level survey samples small; treat district rates with the same caution as noted in the Technical Rules regarding sparsely populated districts.

## Extraction Notes

- Always anchor a figure to its administrative level (district/territory) and reference year.
- Condensed "in Figures" booklets are quick-reference summaries; for full time series and district breakdowns, the main Development Statistics volume is the authoritative source.
- GB is a distinct administrative territory, not a province; preserve this label when citing figures.
